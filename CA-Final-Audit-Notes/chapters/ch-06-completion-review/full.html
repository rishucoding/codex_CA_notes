Chapter map

Completion & Review is the pre-sign-off loop. Substantive fieldwork is done; the auditor now accumulates leftover misstatements (SA 450), chases post-balance-sheet events (SA 560), tests the going-concern assumption (SA 570), collects written reps from management/TCWG (SA 580), sanity-checks comparatives (SA 710), reads other information in the annual report (SA 720), closes the loop with TCWG (SA 260), and hands the file to the reporting phase (Ch 7).

Completion-phase cluster

Completion-phase theme — what happens AFTER evidence, BEFORE signing

Think of a five-item checklist the engagement partner walks through on the last day of fieldwork: (1) have all uncorrected misstatements been accumulated and evaluated (SA 450); (2) have events between year-end and report-date been adjusted/disclosed (SA 560); (3) does the going-concern basis still hold (SA 570); (4) is the written-representation letter signed and dated as near as practicable to the audit report (SA 580); (5) do comparatives (SA 710) and other information (SA 720) tell a consistent story. Only then does the auditor move into opinion-formation under SA 700/705/706.

SA 710 Comparative Information — Corresponding Figures and Comparative Financial Statements

Scope: Auditor's responsibility regarding comparative information in an FS audit. Two approaches exist — the FRF and terms of engagement dictate which applies. (Verbatim text sourced from ch-07.pdf §10; ch-06.pdf does not reproduce SA 710.)

Objective

"The objectives of the auditor are to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence about whether the comparative information included in the financial statements has been presented, in all material respects, in accordance with the requirements for comparative information in the applicable financial reporting framework; and to report in accordance with the auditor's reporting responsibilities."

Key Definitions

  • Comparative information — amounts & disclosures of the prior period presented for comparative purposes
  • Corresponding figures (CF) — prior-period amounts shown as an integral part of current-period FS; opinion refers to current period only
  • Comparative financial statements (CFS) — prior-period amounts/disclosures presented for comparison with current period; opinion refers to each period presented
  • Predecessor auditor — auditor of the prior-period FS now replaced

Key Requirements

  • Determine whether FS contain appropriately classified comparatives — agree to prior-period amounts, consistent accounting policies, changes properly disclosed
  • If possible misstatement surfaces in comparatives: perform additional procedures; if prior period audited, follow SA 560 mechanics
  • Obtain written representation (SA 580) — including a specific rep on any prior-period item disclosed in the current year
  • CF approach: opinion shall not refer to corresponding figures except in the two trigger situations (see exceptions)
  • CFS approach: opinion refers to each period for which FS are presented
  • Prior period audited by predecessor / unaudited → Other Matter Paragraph disclosure (see reporting)

Exceptions / Conditions — when current opinion must touch the prior period

CF approach — refer to CF only if: (a) prior-period report was other than unqualified and the matter remains unresolved; or (b) auditor obtains sufficient evidence that a material misstatement exists in prior-period FS on which an unmodified opinion was previously issued. Use SA 705 + Other Matter Paragraph (SA 706).
CFS approach: if current-period opinion on the prior period differs from the previously issued opinion, disclose substantive reasons in an Other Matter Paragraph.
Unaudited prior period: state in Other Matter that comparatives/CF are unaudited — does not relieve the auditor from obtaining SAAE that opening balances do not contain material misstatements affecting current period (link to SA 510).

Auditor's Responsibilities — procedure snapshot

  • Check consistency of accounting policies; tick comparative amounts to prior-period figures
  • Evaluate any restatement / reclassification; verify disclosure of the change
  • If predecessor auditor & law permits reference — state name, type of opinion, date of prior report

SA 720 (Revised) The Auditor's Responsibilities Relating to Other Information

Scope: Auditor's responsibilities relating to other information — financial or non-financial (other than FS & the auditor's report thereon) — included in the entity's annual report. Does not apply to preliminary announcements or securities-offering documents (prospectuses). (Verbatim text sourced from ch-07.pdf §11; ch-06.pdf does not reproduce SA 720.)

Objective

"The objectives of the auditor, having read the other information, are: (a) To consider whether there is a material inconsistency between the other information and the financial statements; (b) To consider whether there is a material inconsistency between the other information and the auditor's knowledge obtained in the audit; (c) To respond appropriately when the auditor identifies that such material inconsistencies appear to exist, or when the auditor otherwise becomes aware that other information appears to be materially misstated; and (d) To report in accordance with this SA."

Key Definitions

  • Annual report — document(s) prepared on annual basis by management/TCWG providing owners with info on operations & financial results; accompanies FS & auditor's report; typically includes developments, outlook, risks, governance
  • Other information — financial or non-financial information (other than FS & auditor's report thereon) included in the annual report
  • Material inconsistency — other information contradicts FS or the auditor's audit knowledge such that credibility is undermined
  • Material misstatement of other information — OI incorrectly stated or otherwise misleading, including due to omission/obscuring

Key Requirements — the 4-step workflow

  • Obtain: discuss with management what comprises the annual report, agree timing to receive final version prior to report date where possible; if OI will be received after → written rep from management that final version will be provided
  • Read & consider: compare selected items in OI with FS; consider inconsistency vs auditor's audit knowledge; remain alert for OI unrelated to FS that appears misstated
  • Respond: if inconsistency/misstatement appears to exist — discuss with management; perform other procedures; conclude whether (a) OI misstated, (b) FS misstated, or (c) auditor's understanding needs update
  • If OI materially misstated: request correction; if refused → communicate with TCWG; still uncorrected → consider implications for report and withdraw where law permits
  • If misstatement surfaces AFTER report date: if corrected — perform necessary procedures; if not corrected after TCWG communication — take appropriate action to bring it to users' attention
  • Report: separate "Other Information" section in auditor's report when (a) listed entity — OI obtained or expected; (b) unlisted — OI obtained

Exceptions / Conditions

SA 720 does not apply to preliminary announcements of financial information or to securities-offering documents (including prospectuses).
When a qualified or adverse opinion is expressed under SA 705, consider the implications of the underlying matter for the Other Information statement — the "nothing to report" line cannot stand unaffected.

Contents of the Other Information section

  • Statement that management is responsible for the OI
  • Identification of OI obtained before report date, and (for listed) OI expected after
  • Statement that auditor's opinion does not cover OI — no assurance conclusion expressed
  • Description of auditor's responsibilities — to read, consider, report
  • Where OI obtained before report date: either "nothing to report" or description of uncorrected material misstatement of OI

Conclusion / Reporting

The OI section sits alongside (not inside) the opinion paragraph — it informs users that the auditor has read OI but does not audit it. When OI is materially misstated and uncorrected, the auditor describes the misstatement in this section — opinion on the FS remains driven by SA 705 on its own basis.

Revisit cards — completion-phase SAs owned by other chapters

Short scope + mechanic + pointer. Full treatment in the primary chapter.

SA 450 Evaluation of Misstatements Identified during the Audit (revisit)

Scope: Accumulate, classify, communicate & evaluate uncorrected misstatements at completion.

Mechanic (1-liner): Accumulate (except clearly trivial) → request correction → reassess materiality under SA 320 → evaluate individually & in aggregate → written rep that uncorrected items are immaterial → modify under SA 705 if not.

Primary chapter: Ch 4 · Materiality, Risk & Internal Control — full requirements, 4-step loop, communication thresholds.

SA 560 Subsequent Events (revisit)

Scope: Events between date of FS and date of auditor's report + facts known after.

Mechanic (1-liner): Three windows — (i) between FS date & report date: active procedures to identify adjusting / non-adjusting events; (ii) after report date but before FS issued: discuss, amend auditor's report with additional date / new report; (iii) after FS issued: review management action, re-issue report if amended, else take steps to prevent reliance.

Primary chapter: Ch 5 · Audit Evidence. The Ch 6 PDF reproduces the SA 560 pictorial but defers depth to Intermediate.

SA 570 (Revised) Going Concern (revisit)

Scope: Appropriateness of management's use of going-concern basis & material uncertainty.

Mechanic (1-liner): Management assesses → auditor obtains SAAE → four reporting branches: (a) basis inappropriate → adverse; (b) basis appropriate but material uncertainty adequately disclosed → unmodified + separate "Material Uncertainty Related to Going Concern" section; (c) uncertainty inadequately disclosed → qualified/adverse with note in Basis of Opinion; (d) management unwilling to assess / extend → consider implications for report (possibly disclaim).

Primary chapter: Ch 5 · Audit Evidence (whitelisted flowchart lives there). Note from Ch 6 PDF: SA 570 too is an Intermediate-depth SA — at Final, case-study application only.

SA 580 Written Representations (revisit)

Scope: Written reps from management and, where appropriate, TCWG.

Mechanic (1-liner): Necessary but not sufficient audit evidence; date as near as practicable to, but not after, the date of the auditor's report; cover all periods referred to in the report; if unreliable or refused → re-evaluate integrity, consider impact on opinion under SA 705 (possibly disclaimer where refusal concerns FS responsibilities).

Primary chapter: Ch 5 · Audit Evidence. Frequent exam anchor via case scenarios — stale-dated rep letter, missing prior-period reps for restated FS (IPO scenario).

SA 260 (Revised) Communication with TCWG (revisit)

Scope: Two-way communication with those charged with governance at completion.

Mechanic (1-liner): Before signing, communicate qualitative aspects of accounting practices, significant difficulties, uncorrected misstatements, expected modifications / EOM / OM paragraphs, auditor independence (listed), matters requiring written reps — in writing where oral would be inadequate.

Primary chapter: Ch 2 · General Auditing Principles. Inadequate two-way communication → modify risk assessment or consider withdrawal.

Written representations — dating rules

  • Date: as near as practicable to, but not after, the date of the auditor's report
  • Cover: all periods referred to in the auditor's report (even where current management was not in place — their responsibility for FS as a whole is undiminished)
  • Content pillars: management's FS responsibility · completeness of information provided · access to records & people · assertions (SA 240 fraud · SA 250 NOCLAR · SA 450 uncorrected MS · SA 550 related parties · SA 560 subsequent events · SA 570 GC plans)
  • If requested rep not provided — discuss, re-evaluate management integrity, consider impact on audit opinion (disclaimer where concerns FS as a whole)

5 Exam Triggers — spot these phrasings, invoke these SAs

  1. "Prior-period FS were qualified for inventory-valuation; the matter remains unresolved in the current year; the auditor is using the corresponding figures approach" → SA 710 — CF approach triggers reference to corresponding figures; modify under SA 705 and insert an Other Matter Paragraph disclosing the unresolved prior-period modification.
  2. "Chairman's report in the annual report cites ₹ 210 cr net profit; audited FS show ₹ 185 cr; management refuses to correct" → SA 720 — material inconsistency; communicate with TCWG; if still uncorrected, describe the uncorrected material misstatement of OI in the Other Information section of the audit report; consider withdrawal where permitted.
  3. "Management Representation Letter is dated 15-Apr-2024; audit report dated 31-Jul-2024; company completed a material acquisition on 10-Jun-2024" → SA 580 · SA 560 — stale rep letter violates "as near as practicable to, but not after"; obtain refreshed rep dated close to report date; confirm subsequent-event coverage through 31-Jul-2024.
  4. "Listed entity; 2024-25 financial results presented as comparative; the auditor's current opinion on 2023-24 differs from the previously issued opinion on 2023-24" → SA 710 (CFS approach) — disclose substantive reasons for the different opinion on prior period in an Other Matter Paragraph.
  5. "Between report date and FS issuance, a ₹ 2 cr civil suit is lost by the client; management agrees to amend FS" → SA 560 Window 2 — carry out procedures on the amendment; issue a new / dual-dated report; date of new report not earlier than the date of approval of the amended FS; EOM/OM restricted to the amendment is permissible.
