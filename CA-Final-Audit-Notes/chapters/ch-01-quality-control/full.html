Chapter 1 — Quality Control

ICAI SM Final P3 May 2026 · ~8–10 marks Covers SQC 1 (firm level) · SA 220 (engagement level)

Chapter map

Two standards, one hierarchy. SQC 1 is the firm-level umbrella — the firm builds a system of quality control for every audit, review and related-services engagement it takes. SA 220 is the engagement-level echo — the engagement partner implements those policies on a specific audit. SA 220 is explicitly premised on the basis that the firm is subject to SQC 1 — the team may rely on the firm's system unless information suggests otherwise. The PDF closes with the three review mechanisms that sit outside the firm: Peer Review Board, Quality Review Board, and NFRA.

SQC 1 Quality Control for Firms that Perform Audits and Reviews of Historical Financial Information, and Other Assurance and Related Services Engagements

Scope: Firm-level system of quality control. Applies to all firms irrespective of constitution. Covers audits, reviews of HFI, other assurance engagements, and related-services engagements (e.g. compilation).

Objective

"SQC 1 requires that the firm should establish a system of quality control designed to provide it with reasonable assurance that the firm and its personnel comply with professional standards and regulatory and legal requirements and that reports issued by the firm or engagement partners are appropriate in the circumstances."

Key Definitions

  • System of quality control — firm-wide policies & procedures covering the six elements
  • Engagement partner — partner/other person in the firm responsible for an engagement & its performance, for the report issued, and who has appropriate authority
  • Engagement quality control review (EQCR) — objective evaluation of significant judgments & conclusions before report issue
  • Suitably qualified external person — individual outside firm with capabilities & competence of an engagement partner (e.g. partner/experienced employee of another firm)
  • Familiarity threat — long-association risk; senior personnel on assurance engagement over prolonged period may impair quality

Key Requirements — six elements (must-memorise)

  • Leadership responsibilities for quality within the firm — tone at top, CEO/managing partners, commercial considerations must not override quality
  • Ethical requirements — Code of Ethics, independence, annual written confirmation, familiarity-threat safeguards
  • Acceptance & continuance of client relationships & specific engagements — integrity, competence, ethics; document resolution of issues; withdrawal protocol
  • Human resources — recruitment, training, CPD, performance evaluation; each engagement assigned to an engagement partner with competence/authority/time
  • Engagement performance — briefing, supervision, consultation, EQCR, differences-of-opinion resolution, 60-day assembly, ≥ 7-year retention
  • Monitoring — ongoing evaluation + periodic inspection of completed engagements; deal with complaints; remedial action

Ethics — independence & familiarity threat

  • Policies must enable firm to communicate independence requirements, identify & evaluate threats, take action — eliminate, reduce via safeguards, or withdraw
  • All breaches of independence promptly notified to the firm
  • At least annually, written confirmation of compliance from all personnel required to be independent
  • Criteria for familiarity-threat safeguards consider (a) nature of engagement incl. public-interest extent, (b) length of service of senior personnel
  • Safeguards e.g. rotation of senior personnel or requiring EQCR
For financial-statement audits of listed entities, the engagement partner must be rotated after a pre-defined period — normally not more than seven years (except where the audit of a listed entity is conducted by a sole practitioner; in that case, mandatory peer review of the firm is the safeguard).

Acceptance & continuance — what the firm must decide

  • Integrity of client, promoters, KMP — identity & reputation of principal owners, KMP, related parties, TCWG; nature of operations; aggressive accounting interpretation / IC attitude; abnormally low-fee pressure; inappropriate scope limitation; money-laundering / criminal-activity indications; reasons for non-reappointment of previous firm
  • Competence — industry knowledge; experience with regulatory/reporting requirements; sufficient personnel; experts; EQCR-eligible individual; ability to complete within deadline
  • Compliance with ethical requirements — including independence
  • Conflict of interest resolved before acceptance
  • If firm learns info post-acceptance that would have caused decline → consider (a) professional/legal duties (including duty to report) & (b) withdrawal from engagement and/or client relationship — discuss with management & TCWG, consider regulatory duty to report withdrawal, document issues, consultations, conclusions & basis

Engagement performance — consultation, EQCR, documentation

  • Consultation on difficult/contentious matters — internal or external; document issues & results; implement conclusions
  • EQCR mandatory for all listed-entity FS audits; firm devises criteria for other engagements requiring EQCR
  • EQCR for listed-entity audit evaluates: team's independence evaluation; significant risks & responses; materiality & significant-risk judgments; consultations on differences of opinion; significance/disposition of corrected & uncorrected misstatements; matters to communicate to management/TCWG/regulators; adequacy of working papers supporting conclusions; appropriateness of the report
  • EQCR reviewer — partner, other firm member (must be ICAI member), suitably qualified external person, or a team; must maintain objectivity; does not participate in engagement or make decisions for the team; may be consulted by engagement partner but significant consultation requires replacement safeguards
  • Differences of opinion — report issued only after resolution; if EQCR recommendations not accepted and not resolved, matter is resolved via firm procedures (consult another practitioner/firm or professional/regulatory body)
  • Assembly of final file on timely basis — not more than 60 days after auditor's report date for audits (other engagements: appropriate limits)
  • Where two reports issued on same subject matter, treat each as separate engagement for assembly timing
  • Confidentiality, safe custody, integrity, accessibility, retrievability of engagement documentation
  • EQCR documentation: (a) required procedures performed, (b) EQCR completed on or before date of auditor's report, (c) reviewer not aware of unresolved matters that would cause belief significant judgments/conclusions inappropriate
  • Engagement documentation is the property of the firm (unless law specifies otherwise); retention for audits ≥ seven years from date of auditor's report (or group auditor's report, if later)

Monitoring

  • Ongoing consideration & evaluation + periodic inspection of a selection of completed engagements
  • Reflect new professional standards & regulatory developments in the QC policies
  • Entrust monitoring to a partner or other person with sufficient experience & authority
  • Deal with complaints & allegations of non-compliance; take remedial action against personnel; address design/operational deficiencies

Conclusion / Reporting

SQC 1 governs the firm's system, not a single report. QC policies & procedures must be documented and communicated to personnel; firm encourages feedback and concerns from staff.

SA 220 Quality Control for an Audit of Financial Statements

Scope: Engagement-level quality control. Applies to audit engagements only. Premised on the basis that the firm is subject to SQC 1; engagement teams are entitled to rely on the firm's system unless information suggests otherwise.

Objective

"The objective of the auditor is to implement quality control procedures at the engagement level that provide the auditor with reasonable assurance that: (a) The audit complies with professional standards and regulatory and legal requirements and (b) The auditor's report issued is appropriate in the circumstances."

Key Definitions

  • Engagement partner — responsible for the audit engagement and its performance, and for the auditor's report issued on behalf of the firm
  • Engagement team — all personnel performing the engagement, including any auditor's experts engaged by the firm
  • EQCR reviewer — partner, other person in firm, suitably qualified external person, or team — sufficient & appropriate technical expertise, experience and authority to objectively evaluate significant judgments & conclusions
  • SA 220 is modelled on lines of SQC 1 — six elements: leadership · ethics · acceptance/continuance · assignment of teams · engagement performance · monitoring

Key Requirements — engagement partner's six responsibilities

  • Leadership responsibilities — take responsibility for overall quality; emphasise complying with standards & firm policies; appropriate report; and team's ability to raise concerns without fear of reprisals
  • Relevant ethical requirements — identify threats to independence that safeguards may not eliminate / reduce; report to relevant persons within firm; eliminate activity/interest or withdraw (where legally permitted)
  • Acceptance & continuance — satisfy that appropriate procedures under SQC 1 have been followed; conclusions are appropriate based on integrity of principal owners, team competence, ethical compliance, significant matters
  • Assignment of engagement teams — team + experts collectively have appropriate competence & capabilities for the engagement
  • Engagement performance — direction, supervision & performance; review of documentation before report; SAAE supports conclusions & report; appropriate consultation on difficult/contentious matters
  • Monitoring — consider results of firm's monitoring process and whether deficiencies noted may affect the audit engagement

EQCR — engagement partner's listed-entity duties

For audits of listed entities (and any other engagement the firm has determined EQCR-required), the engagement partner shall: (a) determine that an EQCR reviewer has been appointed; (b) discuss significant matters including those identified during EQCR with the reviewer; (c) not date the auditor's report until completion of the EQCR.

EQCR reviewer's objective evaluation — what to do

  • Discussion of significant matters with engagement partner
  • Review of the financial statements and the proposed auditor's report
  • Review of selected audit documentation relating to significant judgments and conclusions reached
  • Evaluation of conclusions reached in formulating the auditor's report and whether the proposed report is appropriate

Listed-entity EQCR reviewer — additional considerations

  • Engagement team's evaluation of firm's independence for the engagement
  • Whether appropriate consultation occurred on differences of opinion / difficult or contentious matters, and conclusions thereon
  • Whether audit documentation selected reflects work performed on significant judgments and supports conclusions reached

Differences of Opinion

If differences arise within team, with those consulted, or between engagement partner & EQCR reviewer, team follows firm's policies/procedures (per SQC 1) to resolve. Report shall not be issued until the difference is resolved — if EQCR recommendations not accepted, matter is escalated (another practitioner/firm or professional/regulatory body).

Documentation — engagement partner shall document

  • Issues identified on ethical compliance & how resolved
  • Conclusions on independence compliance + relevant discussions with firm
  • Conclusions on acceptance & continuance of client relationships / audit engagements
  • Nature & scope of, and conclusions from, consultations undertaken during the engagement

EQCR reviewer documents that (a) required procedures performed, (b) EQCR completed on or before date of auditor's report, (c) not aware of unresolved matters causing belief that team's significant judgments/conclusions were inappropriate.

Conclusion / Reporting

SA 220 does not produce a separate report — it shapes the single auditor's report already mandated by SA 700. Its practical effect: no listed-entity audit report is dated before EQCR completion; where EQCR recommendations remain unresolved, report is withheld pending firm-level resolution.

Special focus — exam-favourite mechanics

Engagement Quality Control Review (EQCR) — pocket card

  • Mandatory for: all FS audits of listed entities. For other engagements — firm's own criteria.
  • Purpose: objective evaluation of significant judgments & conclusions before report issue. Does not reduce the engagement partner's responsibility.
  • Who: partner / other firm member (ICAI member) / suitably qualified external person / team. For listed-entity audit — EQCR must have experience & authority of an audit engagement partner on listed entities.
  • Timing: must be completed before the report is dated (SA 220). Under SA 700, report date cannot be earlier than SAAE obtained.
  • Objectivity shield: EQCR does not participate in the engagement or make team decisions. Engagement partner may consult EQCR without compromising objectivity; where consultation becomes significant, firm may replace EQCR or the consultee.

Familiarity threat — rotation rule

  • Using the same senior personnel on an assurance engagement over a prolonged period may impair quality.
  • Criteria for safeguards — (i) nature of engagement including extent to which it involves a matter of public interest, (ii) length of service of senior personnel.
  • Safeguards — rotation of senior personnel or requiring an EQCR.
  • For listed-entity FS audits — engagement partner rotated after pre-defined period, normally not more than 7 years. Sole-practitioner listed audits → exception, but mandatory peer review applies.

File-assembly & retention timelines

  • Final engagement file assembly — not more than 60 days after auditor's report date for audit engagements.
  • Retention period for audit engagements — ≥ 7 years from date of auditor's report (or group auditor's report, if later).
  • Two reports on same subject matter → each report's assembly timing computed separately.
  • Post-assembly — no deletion/alteration to conceal; integrity, accessibility, retrievability preserved.

Acceptance & continuance — fact-pattern checklist

  • Principal owners / KMP / TCWG reputation & integrity — red flags: ED/IT searches, money-laundering leads, bogus capital-gain shelters
  • Previous auditor — reasons for non-reappointment / resignation; is there a casual vacancy engineered by fee-hikes?
  • Aggressive accounting attitude / inappropriate scope limitation
  • Abnormally low-fee pressure — or abnormally high fee offered as a lure
  • Firm competence / capacity — new industry, new geography, new regulator, experienced staff availability
  • Conflicts of interest / familial connections of partners with client owners — independence assessment
  • If accepted despite issues → document how issues were resolved
  • If later info would have caused decline → withdrawal protocol (discuss with management/TCWG, consider regulator duty, document)

5 Exam Triggers — spot these phrasings, invoke these standards

  1. "Managing partner's family friend is the promoter; previous auditor resigned; client offers 25% fee hike; client under regulatory inquiry — MP pushes firm to accept" → SQC 1 · Acceptance & Continuance + Leadership: commercial considerations must not override quality; independence of MP threatened; integrity red flags; firm should decline or document resolution.
  2. "Paid-employee CA sent by firm for stock audit uses it to solicit statutory audit and promises a clean report as quid pro quo" → SQC 1 · Ethical Requirements: violates Code of Ethics; reflects failure to communicate QC policies to personnel; reports are not appropriate.
  3. "Audit report of listed co. dated 15 May; EQCR completed 18 May; engagement partner had reviewed by 12 May" → SA 220 · EQCR: engagement partner must not date report until EQCR is complete; date of report is incorrect; contravenes SA 220 & SA 700.
  4. "EQCR reviewer disagrees with engagement partner on revenue recognition / onerous contracts provision; EP unwilling to accept recommendation" → SQC 1 & SA 220 · Differences of Opinion: report issued only after resolution; escalate per firm procedure (consult another practitioner/firm or professional/regulatory body).
  5. "CARO clause vii(a) report of listed co. says company is 'generally regular' in depositing statutory dues" → SA 220 · Objective: QC procedures at engagement level must give reasonable assurance audit complies with regulatory & legal requirements; vague CARO wording fails — reporting not qualitative, audit does not comply.

Key takeaways — 15-second recap

  • SQC 1 = firm (all engagements); SA 220 = engagement partner (audits only); SA 220 presupposes SQC 1.
  • Six-element spine — Leadership · Ethics · Acceptance & Continuance · HR (Assignment) · Engagement Performance · Monitoring.
  • Engagement-partner rotation — ≤ 7 years for listed-entity audits (sole-practitioner carve-out + peer review).
  • EQCR mandatory for all listed-entity FS audits; report not dated until EQCR complete.
  • File assembly ≤ 60 days; retention ≥ 7 years from auditor's report date.
  • External review: PRB (ICAI) · QRB (non-NFRA companies) · NFRA (listed / banks / insurers / Rule 3).
