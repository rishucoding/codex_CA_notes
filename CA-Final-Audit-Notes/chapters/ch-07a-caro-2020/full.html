Chapter 7a — CARO 2020 (Companies (Auditor's Report) Order)

ICAI SM Final P3 May 2026 · ~6–10 marks · 21 clauses under s.143(11) of Companies Act, 2013

Chapter map

CARO 2020 is an additional reporting requirement attached to every statutory audit of companies to which the Order applies. It is not an SA — it is a Central Government Order issued under s.143(11), Companies Act, 2013, dated 25 Feb 2020, after consultation with NFRA (s.132). The output of CARO is a separate statement annexed to the main audit report under the head "Report on Other Legal and Regulatory Requirements".

The Order has 21 clauses in Para 3 plus a reason-to-state requirement in Para 4 for every unfavourable/qualified answer. The student's job at Final: know which clauses apply to which fact pattern, the reporting keyword, and the common trap — especially title deeds, related-party loans, statutory dues, default in repayment, cash losses, CSR unspent, and the CFS-only clause (xxi).

1. Source hook — s.143(11) and the Order

"In exercise of the powers conferred by section 143(11) of the Companies Act, 2013, the Central Government, after consultation with the National Financial Reporting Authority constituted under section 132 of the Companies Act, 2013, has issued the Companies (Auditor's Report) Order, 2020, (CARO, 20) dated 25th February, 2020."
  • Power source: s.143(11) — Central Govt may direct auditor's report to include additional matters
  • Consultation: NFRA (s.132)
  • Structure: Para 1 applicability · Para 2 hook to s.143 · Para 3 21 clauses · Para 4 reasons for unfavourable/qualified answers

2. Applicability — who is in, who is out

The Order applies to every company including a foreign company (s.2(42)) audited under s.143, except five exempted classes. Exemption is not a blanket company-size test — conditions matter (especially for private limited).

Exempted classes — Para 1 of the Order

  1. Banking company as defined in s.5(c) BR Act, 1949
  2. Insurance company as defined in the Insurance Act, 1938
  3. Company licensed to operate under s.8 of the Companies Act
  4. One Person Company (s.2(62)) and small company (s.2(85))
  5. A private limited company, not being a subsidiary or holding of a public company, satisfying all of: paid-up capital + reserves & surplus ≤ ₹1 crore on BS date · total borrowings from any bank/FI at no point during the FY > ₹1 crore · total revenue (incl. discontinuing) as per Sch III ≤ ₹10 crore during the FY
Private Ltd test is cumulative — failing any one of the three limits (capital, borrowings, revenue) means CARO applies. And a private limited co. that is a subsidiary or holding of a public company is never eligible for this exemption.

3. Hook to the audit report — Para 2

"Every report made by the auditor under section 143 of the Companies Act, 2013 on the accounts of every company audited by him, to which this Order applies, for the financial year, shall in addition, contain the matters specified in paragraphs 3 and 4, as may be applicable."

The CARO statement is therefore attached to the main s.143 report, typically under "Report on Other Legal and Regulatory Requirements".

"It may be noted that the Order shall not apply to the auditor's report on consolidated financial statements except clause (xxi) of paragraph 3."

5. Para 4 — reasons for unfavourable / qualified answers

"Where, in the auditor's report, the answer to any of the questions referred to in paragraph 3 is unfavourable or qualified, the auditor's report shall also state the basis for such unfavourable or qualified answer, as the case may be."
"Where the auditor is unable to express any opinion on any specified matter, his report shall indicate such fact together with the reasons as to why it is not possible for him to give his opinion on the same."
CARO answers are not "Yes/No" — every unfavourable / qualified / unable-to-opine answer must carry the reason. Bare adverse comments without basis = non-compliance with Para 4.

6. CARO vs CFS — the clause (xxi) rule

The Order's default is that CARO does not apply to the auditor's report on consolidated financial statements. The sole exception is clause (xxi), which requires the holding company's auditor to report — in the CFS CARO — any qualifications or adverse remarks found in the respective CARO reports of the companies included in the CFS, and to list:

  • Names of the companies concerned
  • The paragraph number(s) of the relevant CARO report containing the qualification / adverse remark
No need to re-examine the component's facts — pick up the component auditor's qualifications from their CARO reports. But if component CARO is unavailable, disclose that fact.

7. Clause drill-downs — exam favourites

Clause (i) — PPE, intangibles, title deeds, revaluation, benami

  • (a) Records with quantitative details & situation of PPE; records of intangible assets
  • (b) Physical verification at reasonable intervals; material discrepancies dealt with in books
  • (c) Title deeds of all immovable properties (other than where company is lessee with duly executed lease) in company's name; if not, disclose in prescribed table — description, gross carrying value, name held, whether promoter/director/relative/employee, period held, reason, dispute flag
  • (d) If PPE (incl. ROU) or intangibles revalued — whether by Registered Valuer; state amount of change where aggregate change ≥ 10% of each class
  • (e) Benami proceedings initiated / pending — whether properly disclosed in FS
Fact-pattern hint: director "exchanges" his land with company land twice the size → clause (i)(c) title-deed disclosure + clause (xiii) s.188 RPT + clause (xv) s.192 non-cash with director.

Clause (iii) — Loans, investments, guarantees, security

  • (a) Loans / advances / guarantees / security to entities — aggregate & outstanding split between subsidiaries, JVs, associates vs others [not applicable if principal business is lending]
  • (b) Terms not prejudicial to company's interest
  • (c) Repayment schedule stipulated; receipts regular
  • (d) Overdue > 90 days; reasonable steps for recovery
  • (e) Evergreening — loans renewed / extended / fresh loans to settle existing overdues to same parties; state aggregate & % of total [lender-companies excluded]
  • (f) Loans repayable on demand / without specifying any terms — aggregate & % & amount to promoters & related parties (s.2(76))

Clause (vii) — Statutory dues

  • (a) Undisputed dues — GST, PF, ESI, income-tax, sales-tax, service-tax, customs, excise, VAT, cess & any other statutory dues; arrears outstanding > 6 months from due date — state extent as on last day of FY
  • (b) Disputed dues — amounts & forum where dispute pending; "a mere representation to the concerned Department shall not be treated as a dispute"
A TDS demand of ₹25 lacs raised by DCIT(TDS), not paid, appeal filed under e-appeals with JCIT(Appeals) → is a dispute pending in a legitimate forum → report under clause (vii)(b) with amount & forum.

Clause (ix) — Default in repayment & allied fund-use

  • (a) Default in repayment of loans / other borrowings / interest — report in prescribed format (nature, lender-wise for banks/FIs/Govt., amount, principal/interest, days of delay, remarks)
  • (b) Whether declared wilful defaulter by any bank / FI / lender
  • (c) Term loan applied for stated purpose; if diverted, amount and actual purpose
  • (d) Short-term funds used for long-term purposes — nature & amount
  • (e) Funds taken to meet obligations of subsidiaries / associates / JVs — nature & amount
  • (f) Loans raised on pledge of securities held in subsidiaries / JVs / associates — details & default, if any

Clause (xvii) — Cash losses

Whether the company has incurred cash losses in the financial year and in the immediately preceding financial year; if so, state the amount of cash losses.

"Cash loss" here = loss before non-cash items (chiefly depreciation & amortisation). Report only if losses in both years — current and prior.

Clause (xix) — Ability to meet liabilities within 1 year

On the basis of (a) financial ratios, (b) ageing and expected dates of realisation of financial assets and payment of financial liabilities, (c) other information accompanying the FS, (d) auditor's knowledge of Board & management plans — whether the auditor is of the opinion that no material uncertainty exists as on the date of the audit report that the company is capable of meeting its liabilities existing at the BS date as & when they fall due within 1 year from BS date.

Clause (xix) is not a substitute for SA 570 (Revised) going-concern procedures. Going-concern inappropriate → SA 705 adverse opinion + clause (xix) negative report + SA 570 material-uncertainty paragraph.

Clause (xx) — CSR unspent amounts

  • (a) In respect of other than ongoing projects — whether unspent amount transferred to a Fund specified in Schedule VII within 6 months of expiry of FY per second proviso to s.135(5)
  • (b) In respect of ongoing projects — whether unspent amount transferred to Special Account per s.135(6)
Clause (xx) does not ask whether the 2%-of-avg-profit spend itself was met — only whether unspent amounts were routed correctly. Substantive non-spending is a s.135 board-report matter.

Clause (xxi) — CFS CARO remarks

In the CFS CARO — indicate details of companies (names) and the paragraph numbers of the CARO reports of those companies containing qualifications or adverse remarks.

Only clause of CARO applicable on CFS. Not-in-SM flag: if component auditor's CARO is unavailable / not yet issued, SM does not prescribe the exact template — refer bare Order for practitioner guidance.

8. Reporting language — sample skeletons

  • Clean answer: "In our opinion and according to the information and explanations given to us, clause is not applicable / the company is regular / the records are adequate."
  • Qualified answer: state facts → state the deviation → quantify → reference Para 4 basis.
  • Unable to opine: state fact + reasons why no opinion possible (Para 4(b)).
CARO statement sits inside the audit report under "Report on Other Legal and Regulatory Requirements". Signature block, UDIN & date flow from the main SA 700 report — not signed separately.

📌 5 Exam Triggers — CARO 2020

  1. "Director's adjacent plot swapped for company plot of double size, deed registered by company representatives" → CARO (i)(c) title-deed disclosure of immovable property held not in company's name + (xiii) s.188 RPT + (xv) s.192 non-cash with director.
  2. "TDS survey → ₹25 lacs demand as assessee-in-default; company filed appeal with JCIT(A)" → CARO (vii)(b) disputed statutory dues — report amount & forum (appeal pending); not clause (vii)(a) arrears.
  3. "Holding company's CFS includes subsidiary whose CARO carries an adverse remark on clause (ix) default" → CARO (xxi) — holding co. auditor must name the subsidiary & cite the clause-(ix) para number in the CFS CARO; other CARO clauses don't apply on CFS.
  4. "Private Ltd co. (not subsidiary of public co.): paid-up + R&S ₹80 lakh, borrowings ₹90 lakh at peak, revenue ₹9.5 cr — but during year borrowings touched ₹1.2 cr for 2 days" → CARO applies — the ₹1 cr borrowing limit is tested "at any point during the FY"; breach on any day defeats exemption.
  5. "Going-concern basis found inappropriate, management refuses to reflect → SA 705 adverse" → additionally CARO (xix) — report that a material uncertainty exists and the company is not capable of meeting its liabilities due within 1 year; clause (xix) + SA 570 + SA 705 ride together.
