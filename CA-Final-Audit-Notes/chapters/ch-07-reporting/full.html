Chapter 7 — Reporting

ICAI SM Final P3 May 2026 · ~15–20 marks SA 700 · 701 · 705 · 706 · 710 (revisit) · 720 (revisit) · CARO overview

Chapter map

Reporting is the culmination of the audit. SA 700 is the core — how to form the opinion and what the report must contain, with SA 701 bolting on Key Audit Matters for listed entities. SA 705 tells you how to modify when things are wrong, and SA 706 lets you add Emphasis of Matter or Other Matter paragraphs without modifying. SA 710 & SA 720 are primary in Ch 6 — brief revisit only. Finally, CARO 2020 (under s.143(11)) is an additional reporting duty — overview here, 21-clause detail lives in Ch 7a.

From opinion to report — SA cluster

SA 700 Forming an Opinion & Reporting on Financial Statements

Scope: Auditor's responsibility to form an opinion on a complete set of general-purpose FS, and the form & content of the report. Applies to every audit conducted under SAs.

Objective

"(a) To form an opinion on the financial statements based on an evaluation of the conclusions drawn from the audit evidence obtained; and (b) To express clearly that opinion through a written report."

Key Definitions

  • Unmodified opinion — FS prepared, in all material respects, per applicable FRF
  • General-purpose FS — prepared per a general-purpose framework (fair presentation or compliance)
  • Fair-presentation framework — "present fairly" / "true and fair view" phrasing required

Forming the opinion — what the auditor concludes

  • Obtained reasonable assurance FS as a whole are free from material misstatement (fraud or error)
  • Evaluated: accounting policies selected & applied, reasonableness of estimates, adequacy of disclosures
  • For fair-presentation framework: also evaluate overall presentation, structure, content & whether FS achieve fair presentation
  • Sufficient appropriate audit evidence obtained; uncorrected misstatements material individually or aggregate?

Basic Elements of the Auditor's Report (ICAI-prescribed order)

  1. Title — "Independent Auditor's Report"
  2. Addressee — Members (statutory) / Board (special purpose)
  3. Opinion section — identifies entity, FS audited, period, FRF; uses "present fairly" / "true and fair view"
  4. Basis for Opinion — conducted per SAs · independence per ICAI Code of Ethics · SAAE obtained
  5. Going Concern (where applicable, per SA 570)
  6. Key Audit Matters (if SA 701 applies)
  7. Other Information (per SA 720)
  8. Responsibilities of Management & TCWG for the FS
  9. Auditor's Responsibilities for the audit of the FS
  10. Location of description of auditor's responsibilities (body · appendix · website where permitted)
  11. Other Reporting Responsibilities — "Report on Other Legal and Regulatory Requirements" (CARO, s.143(3))
  12. Signature of the auditor (personal name; firm name + FRN; membership no.; UDIN)
  13. Place of Signature
  14. Date — not earlier than date SAAE obtained & FS approved

Going Concern reporting (SA 570) — implications

  • Use of GC basis inappropriate → Adverse opinion
  • GC appropriate + material uncertainty, adequate disclosure → unmodified + separate section "Material Uncertainty Related to Going Concern"
  • Material uncertainty + inadequate disclosure → Qualified / Adverse with basis paragraph
  • Management unwilling to make/extend assessment → consider implications for report

Exceptions / Conditions

Signature on audit report — only the appointed auditor (engagement partner) may sign, per s.145 r/w s.141(2). Cl. 12 Part I Sch. I of CA Act 1949 bars a CA from letting a non-member / non-partner sign on his behalf.
UDIN mandatory (w.e.f. 1 July 2019) — manual & digital reports/certificates.
Supplementary information: if an integral part of FS → covered by opinion; if not an integral part, must be clearly differentiated, else auditor identifies the unaudited supplementary info in the report.

Conclusion / Reporting

Written report expressing unmodified opinion if FS prepared in all material respects per FRF; else modify per SA 705.

SA 701 Communicating Key Audit Matters in the Independent Auditor's Report

Scope: Communication of KAM in the auditor's report — deals with the auditor's judgment on what to communicate and the form/content of that communication.

Objective

"To enhance the communicative value of the auditor's report by providing greater transparency about the audit that was performed. To assist the user in understanding those matters that, in the auditor's professional judgment, were of most significance in the audit of the financial statements of the current period."

Key Definition

  • Key Audit Matters — matters that, in the auditor's professional judgment, were of most significance in the audit of FS of the current period; selected from matters communicated with TCWG.

Applicability

  • Mandatory: complete sets of general-purpose FS of listed entities
  • When law or regulation requires KAM
  • When the auditor otherwise decides voluntarily to communicate KAM

Determining KAM — 3-step funnel

  1. Matters communicated with TCWG →
  2. Those requiring significant auditor attention — considering: (a) areas of higher assessed RoMM / significant risks per SA 315; (b) significant auditor judgments re: areas of significant management judgment & high-estimation-uncertainty estimates; (c) effect of significant events/transactions →
  3. From the above, those of most significance in the current-period audit = KAM

Key Requirements

  • Introductory language: KAM are matters of most significance in the current-period audit · addressed in context of audit as a whole · auditor does not provide a separate opinion on them
  • Each KAM: description + why it was a KAM + how the auditor addressed it in the audit
  • Communicate with TCWG on matters determined to be KAM
  • If no KAM to communicate → say so explicitly in a KAM section

What KAM is NOT (four negatives)

KAM is not: (a) a substitute for disclosures in the FS required by the FRF; (b) a substitute for a modified opinion when SA 705 requires one; (c) a substitute for reporting per SA 570 when a material uncertainty on going concern exists; or (d) a separate opinion on individual matters.

Exceptions / Conditions

Auditor shall not communicate KAM when disclaiming an opinion (unless required by law/regulation). Law/regulation may preclude public disclosure of a matter; in extremely rare circumstances, adverse consequences of disclosure may reasonably be expected to outweigh public-interest benefits — then do not communicate.

Examples of matters that often become KAM

  • Assessment of impairment; provision for losses & contingencies
  • Valuation of financial instruments; revenue recognition
  • Taxation matters — multiple jurisdictions, uncertain positions, deferred tax assets

Conclusion / Reporting

KAM section sits immediately after Basis for Opinion (and Material Uncertainty re GC, if any). KAM is an addition to — never a replacement for — the opinion, modification, or required disclosures.

SA 705 Modifications to the Opinion in the Independent Auditor's Report

Scope: When, in forming an opinion per SA 700, the auditor concludes a modification is necessary — issuing the appropriate report + revised form and content.

Objective

"The objective of the auditor is to express clearly an appropriately modified opinion on the financial statements that is necessary when: (a) the auditor concludes, based on the audit evidence obtained, that the financial statements as a whole are not free from material misstatement; or (b) the auditor is unable to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence to conclude that the financial statements as a whole are free from material misstatement."

Key Definitions

  • Pervasive — effects (or possible effects) that (i) are not confined to specific elements/accounts/items, or (ii) if confined, represent or could represent a substantial proportion of the FS, or (iii) in relation to disclosures, are fundamental to users' understanding
  • Two trigger families: FS materially misstated (evidence obtained) · inability to obtain SAAE (scope limitation)

Key Requirements — when modifying

  • Use heading: "Qualified Opinion" / "Adverse Opinion" / "Disclaimer of Opinion"
  • Amend "Basis for Opinion" → "Basis for Qualified / Adverse / Disclaimer of Opinion" with a description & quantification of the matter (or state if impracticable)
  • For inability to obtain SAAE: include reasons for that inability in the Basis section
  • Qualified/adverse → amend "sufficient & appropriate" statement to add "qualified" / "adverse"
  • Disclaimer → omit the reference to auditor's responsibilities section & the SAAE-sufficient statement; amend auditor's-responsibilities description to short form (engaged-to-audit, could not obtain SAAE, independence statement)
  • Communicate with TCWG: circumstances leading to expected modification & wording

Management-imposed scope limitation (after engagement accepted)

  • Request management to remove the limitation
  • If refused → communicate to TCWG (unless all are in management); try alternative procedures
  • If effects could be material but not pervasive → qualify
  • If effects could be material and pervasive → withdraw where practicable & legal; else disclaim

Exceptions / Conditions

Adverse opinion or disclaimer on FS as a whole → auditor's report shall not also include an unmodified opinion on a single statement/element of the same FS under the same FRF (would contradict).
Disclaimer → no KAM section unless required by law/regulation.
SEBI Oct 18, 2019 circular: listed-entity auditor cannot withdraw without issuing the audit/limited-review report — resignation within 45 days of quarter-end ⇒ issue report for that quarter; after 45 days ⇒ that quarter + next; if 3 quarters done ⇒ full-year report before resigning. ICAI: unlisted auditor cannot cite "professional pre-occupation" as resignation reason.

SA 705 / SA 706 decision tree

flowchart TD A[Opinion being formed under SA 700] --> B{MM identified OR unable to obtain SAAE?} B -->|No — neither| U[Unmodified opinion] B -->|Yes| C{Material?} C -->|No| U C -->|Yes| D{Pervasive?} D -->|No, MM| Q1[Qualified · except for effects] D -->|No, SAAE| Q2[Qualified · except for possible effects] D -->|Yes, MM| AD[Adverse opinion] D -->|Yes, SAAE| DS[Disclaimer of opinion] U --> E{SA 706 add-on?} Q1 --> E Q2 --> E AD --> E DS --> E E -->|Matter IN FS, fundamental to users| EOM[Emphasis of Matter para] E -->|Matter NOT in FS, relevant to audit/auditor/report| OM[Other Matter para] E -->|Neither| END[No extra para]

Conclusion / Reporting

Clearly modified opinion + revised Basis + TCWG communication. Even in adverse / disclaimer, describe in Basis any other matters that would have required modification.

SA 706 Emphasis of Matter & Other Matter Paragraphs in the Independent Auditor's Report

Scope: Additional communication in the report — when the auditor judges it necessary to draw users' attention to a matter. Supplement, never a substitute for modification.

Objective

"The objective of the auditor, having formed an opinion on the financial statements, is to draw users' attention, when in the auditor's judgment it is necessary to do so, by way of clear additional communication in the auditor's report, to: (a) A matter, although appropriately presented or disclosed in the financial statements, that is of such importance that it is fundamental to users' understanding of the financial statements; or (b) As appropriate, any other matter that is relevant to users' understanding of the audit, the auditor's responsibilities or the auditor's report."

Key Definitions

  • Emphasis of Matter paragraph — para in the report that refers to a matter appropriately presented or disclosed in the FS that, in the auditor's judgment, is of such importance that it is fundamental to users' understanding of the FS.
  • Other Matter paragraph — para that refers to a matter other than those presented or disclosed in the FS that, in the auditor's judgment, is relevant to users' understanding of the audit, the auditor's responsibilities, or the auditor's report.

EoM vs OM — one-line rule

TestEmphasis of Matter (EoM)Other Matter (OM)
Matter located…IN the FS (presented / disclosed)NOT in the FS
Why includedFundamental to users' understanding of the FSRelevant to users' understanding of audit / auditor's responsibilities / report
Opinion modified?No — state "opinion is not modified in respect of this matter"No
Typical triggersExceptional litigation uncertainty · significant subsequent event · early adoption of new AS with material effect · major catastrophe · special-purpose frameworkPrior period audited by predecessor auditor (comparatives) · branch FS audited by branch auditors · joint-auditor disagreement cross-reference

Key Requirements — EoM

  • Include within a separate section with a heading that includes "Emphasis of Matter"
  • Clear reference to the matter & to where the related disclosures can be found in the FS — refer only to information presented or disclosed in the FS
  • State that the auditor's opinion is not modified in respect of the matter emphasised
  • Preconditions: modification not required under SA 705, and (where SA 701 applies) the matter has not been determined to be a KAM

Key Requirements — OM

  • Permitted only if not prohibited by law/regulation and, where SA 701 applies, not determined to be a KAM
  • Separate section headed "Other Matter" or similar

Exceptions / Conditions

Not a substitute rule: EoM is not a substitute for a modified opinion under SA 705, a KAM under SA 701, a material-uncertainty report under SA 570, or a disclosure the FRF requires. Widespread use of EoM diminishes its effectiveness.
Must communicate with TCWG regarding the expectation to include an EoM / OM paragraph and the wording.

EoM vs KAM (SA 701) — which goes where?

  • If a matter qualifies as KAM, communicate as KAM — do not use EoM for the same matter
  • If matter is fundamental to user understanding but did not require significant auditor attention (e.g., a subsequent event) → EoM
  • Where KAM section is present, auditor may highlight a matter more prominently (e.g., listed first) instead of duplicating as EoM

Conclusion / Reporting

EoM / OM is a targeted spotlight that preserves an otherwise unmodified (or modified) opinion while flagging a matter. Use sparingly; communicate to TCWG; always state "opinion not modified".

SA 710 Comparative Information — Corresponding Figures & Comparative FS (revisit — primary in Ch 6)

Scope note: Primary coverage in Ch 6. Reporting angle recapped here.

Mechanics (audit procedures for comparatives, written representations, communication with predecessor) live in Ch 6 to avoid duplication. This card only summarises the reporting angle relevant to Ch 7.

Two reporting frameworks — bottom line

  • Corresponding figures: opinion refers to current period only. Do not refer to corresponding figures in the opinion, except when (a) prior AR was other than unqualified, or (b) a prior-period MM was not addressed, or (c) prior-period FS unaudited, or (d) prior period audited by predecessor auditor and auditor (permitted by law) chooses to refer.
  • Comparative financial statements: opinion refers to each period presented. If current-year opinion on prior period differs from the opinion previously issued → explain substantive reason in an Other Matter Paragraph.

Cross-refer: prior period audited by predecessor → OM para stating (i) fact, (ii) type of opinion, (iii) date of that report. Prior-period unaudited → OM para to that effect (does not relieve auditor of duty on opening balances).

SA 720 Auditor's Responsibilities Relating to Other Information (revisit — primary in Ch 6)

Scope note: Primary coverage in Ch 6. Reporting mechanics recapped here.

Read/consider/respond mechanics of SA 720 live in Ch 6. This card covers the "Other Information" section of the auditor's report only.

Reporting — "Other Information" section of the report

  • Required when (a) listed entity: OI obtained or expected to be obtained; (b) unlisted corporate entity: some or all OI obtained
  • Contents: (i) management's responsibility for OI · (ii) identification of OI obtained (and, for listed, expected to be obtained post-report-date) · (iii) auditor's opinion does not cover OI, no assurance expressed · (iv) auditor's SA 720 responsibilities · (v) either "nothing to report" or description of uncorrected material misstatement in OI
  • Modification under SA 705 → consider implications for the OI statement

CARO 2020 — Overview (additional reporting under s.143(11))

Issued by the Central Government, after consultation with NFRA, as the Companies (Auditor's Report) Order, 2020 dated 25 Feb 2020. It is an additional reporting requirement tacked on to the s.143 report — specifically under the "Report on Other Legal and Regulatory Requirements" section.

Applicability — summary

  • Applies to every company (including foreign companies per s.2(42)), except: banking company; insurance company; s.8 company; One Person Company; small company; and private limited company (not subsidiary/holding of a public co.) meeting all thresholds — paid-up capital + reserves ≤ ₹1 cr on BS date · borrowings ≤ ₹1 cr at any point in FY · revenue ≤ ₹10 cr in FY.
  • Does not apply to the auditor's report on consolidated FS — except clause (xxi) (which requires reporting on CARO qualifications/adverse remarks in subsidiaries/associates/JVs consolidated).
CARO 21-clause detail lives in Ch 7a. Clause-by-clause drill-downs (title deeds · inventory · loans to related parties · statutory dues · defaults in repayment · fraud reporting · cash losses · consolidated CARO etc.) are covered in full at chapters/ch-07a-caro-2020/full.html. Do not try to memorise the 21 clauses from this chapter — go to Ch 7a.

Reporting mechanics (para 4)

  • Unfavourable / qualified answer to any paragraph-3 question → state basis for that answer
  • Unable to express any opinion on a matter → indicate the fact + reasons

Auditor's duties under s.143 — quick reference

Ch 7 PDF also recaps statutory duties that drive what goes into the report. Keep these for fact-patterns where the question tests the Companies Act wrapping around SA 700.

Duty to enquire — s.143(1)

  • Loans & advances on security — properly secured? terms prejudicial?
  • Book-entry-only transactions — prejudicial to company?
  • Sale of shares/debentures below cost (non-investment/banking co.)
  • Loans/advances shown as deposits · personal expenses charged to revenue · cash receipt on share allotment

Report must state — s.143(3) (selected)

  • Information & explanations sought/obtained; proper books; branch-auditor reports received & treatment; agreement of BS/P&L with books
  • FS comply with accounting standards; director disqualification under s.164(2); IFC over FR adequacy & operating effectiveness (carve-outs for OPC / small co. / specified private co.)
  • Rule 11 matters — pending litigations impact · long-term contract material foreseeable losses · IEPF transfer delays · "funds loaned/invested through intermediaries" reps (onward & inward) · dividend per s.123 · audit trail (edit log) operated & preserved
  • s.197(16) — statement on managerial remuneration paid per Act, excess if any

Fraud reporting — s.143(12) r/w Rule 13 CAAR 2014

Fraud ≥ ₹1 crore → Central Government (Secretary, MCA): auditor reports to Board/AC within 2 days, seeks reply in 45 days, forwards report + reply (or note if no reply) to CG within 15 days in Form ADT-4.
Fraud < ₹1 crore → report to Audit Committee / Board within 2 days (nature · amount · parties); company discloses in Board's Report (incl. remedial action). Good-faith protection under s.143(13). CARO clause (xi) also requires disclosure of frauds noticed/reported and whether ADT-4 was filed.

Report vs Certificate: "Report" = expression of opinion (s.143), whereas "Certificate" = verification of fact (e.g. s.66 reduction of capital).

Notes on Accounts vs Qualifications — exam-favourite distinction

  • Notes = management's explanation of their stand; Qualifications = auditor's disagreement in the opinion.
  • A reference in the AR to a note does not automatically become a qualification. The auditor must state qualifications independently in the report so users can assess significance.
  • Qualified opinion uses "except for" — never "subject to" or "with the foregoing explanation" (insufficiently forceful).
  • Disagreement requires qualification (or adverse / disclaimer), not an EoM — EoM is only for matters appropriately presented & disclosed and supported by SAAE.

5 Exam Triggers — spot these phrasings, invoke these SAs

  1. "Auditor unable to obtain balance confirmation for debtors = ₹150 cr (defaults ongoing; provision inadequate) and 40% of total assets = inventory, no physical verification, no adequate records" → SA 705 — inability to obtain SAAE, possible effects material & pervasive → Disclaimer of Opinion; no KAM section; shortened auditor's-responsibilities description.
  2. "Company's financing arrangements expired, renegotiation failed, entity considering bankruptcy; FS do not disclose this fact" → SA 705 + SA 570 — material uncertainty on going concern with inadequate disclosure; effects on FS material & pervasive → Adverse Opinion with Basis for Adverse + material uncertainty statement.
  3. "Loan agreement for ₹55 lakhs advance not furnished — auditor cannot verify terms of repayment/interest" → SA 705 — inability to obtain SAAE, material but not pervasive → Qualified Opinion "except for the possible effects of…"; never use EoM.
  4. "Company recognizes complex revenue with rebates/discounts; auditor tested Ind AS 115 compliance, controls, cut-off — listed entity" → SA 701 — disclosure of Key Audit Matter (revenue recognition), describing why it was KAM and how the auditor addressed it. Not an EoM, not a modification.
  5. "Auditor wants to draw attention to (i) Co.'s plan to resume construction on suspended thermal project — already in Note 27; (ii) 5 branch FS audited by branch auditors" → SA 706 — (i) matter in FS & fundamental → Emphasis of Matter; (ii) matter not in FS, relevant to understanding of audit → Other Matter. State "opinion not modified"; do not substitute for a KAM.
