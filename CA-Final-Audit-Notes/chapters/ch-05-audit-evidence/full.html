Chapter map

Audit evidence is the oxygen of the opinion. SA 500 is the umbrella — defines sufficient appropriate audit evidence, the relevance-vs-reliability hierarchy, and the auditor's duty to escalate inconsistencies. The chapter then splits three ways: gathering tools (SA 501 specific items, SA 505 external confirmations, SA 520 analytical procedures, SA 530 sampling); specific balance SAs (SA 510 opening balances, SA 540 estimates, SA 550 related parties, SA 560 subsequent events); and late-stage assertion SAs (SA 570 going concern, SA 580 written representations). Memorise the split — it organises every case question.

SA 500 Audit Evidence

Scope: Umbrella SA on audit evidence — what constitutes it and the auditor's responsibility to design/perform procedures to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence. PDF status (§1.1a): covered in depth at Intermediate; at Final only application/case-study expected.

Objective (from PDF — verbatim)

"To design and perform audit procedures in such a way as to enable the auditor to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence to be able to draw reasonable conclusions on which to base auditor's opinion."

Key Definitions

  • Audit evidence — information used by the auditor in arriving at conclusions on which the opinion is based; includes accounting records & other information
  • Sufficient — measure of quantity; affected by assessed RMM and quality of evidence
  • Appropriate — measure of quality (relevance + reliability)
  • Management's expert — individual/organisation in field other than accounting/auditing, whose work is used by the entity to assist in preparing FS

Key Requirements

  • Consider relevance and reliability of the information used as audit evidence
  • When info comes from a management's expert → evaluate the expert's competence, capabilities & objectivity; obtain understanding of work; evaluate appropriateness as evidence for the relevant assertion
  • When using info produced by the entity → evaluate reliability, completeness, accuracy
  • Obtain evidence via risk assessment procedures + further audit procedures = TOC + substantive (tests of details + substantive analytical)
  • If audit evidence from one source is inconsistent with another, or the auditor has doubts over reliability → determine modifications/additions to procedures needed to resolve the matter & consider effect on other aspects of the audit

Reliability hierarchy (generalisations · all subject to exceptions)

  • Independent external source > internal source
  • Internal evidence when related IC is effective > when IC is weak
  • Evidence obtained directly by the auditor (e.g., observation of a control) > indirectly/inferentially
  • Evidence in documentary form (paper/electronic) > oral
  • Original documents > photocopies / facsimiles / electronic renderings

Management's expert — objectivity caveat (PDF TYK-5)

An expert engaged/employed by the entity cannot ordinarily be regarded as more likely to be objective than other employees. Threats: self-interest · self-review · advocacy · familiarity · intimidation. Evaluate interests (financial, personal, other services) & safeguards. PDF: re-appointment for 5 years by management does not cure the objectivity threat.

Inconsistency / reliability-doubt protocol

Even external-source evidence may be unreliable (all responses carry risk of interception, alteration, fraud; response may not come from the originally intended party — PDF TYK-5 GST-cancelled creditors case). Determine modifications/additions to procedures; escalate as fraud risk factor where appropriate.

Auditor's Responsibilities

  • Design & perform procedures appropriate in the circumstances to obtain SAAE
  • Where entity-prepared information is used, evaluate R/C/A before relying on it
  • Escalate inconsistencies, investigate, document

Conclusion / Reporting

If SAAE cannot be obtained → modify opinion per SA 705 (qualified or disclaimer depending on pervasiveness & materiality).

SA 501 Audit Evidence — Specific Considerations for Selected Items

Scope: Specific considerations in obtaining SAAE for three items — inventory, litigation & claims, segment information. PDF status (§1.1a): Intermediate depth; Final application only.

Objective (from PDF — verbatim)

"To obtain sufficient and appropriate audit evidence regarding: 1. Existence and condition of inventory. 2. Completeness of litigation and claims involving the entity. 3. Presentation and disclosure of segment information in accordance with applicable FRF."

Inventory — procedures

  • If inventory is material: attend physical inventory counting, unless impracticable → (i) evaluate mgmt's instructions & procedures, (ii) observe performance of count, (iii) inspect inventory, (iv) perform test counts
  • Perform audit procedures on the entity's final inventory records to determine they accurately reflect count results
  • Count on date other than FS date → additional procedures for intervening transactions (roll-forward/roll-back)
  • Attendance unforeseeably prevented (PDF TYK-2: NH-7 blockade) → make/observe count on alternate date + procedures on intervening transactions (not a scope limitation)
  • Attendance impracticable (safety, nature, location) → alternative procedures (e.g., inspect documentation of subsequent sale of pre-count inventory); if alternative can't give SAAE → modify opinion (SA 705 scope limitation). Mere inconvenience / cost / time is not impracticability (SA 200 link)
  • Inventory in custody of a third party → confirmation from third party plus inspection / other procedures; if integrity of third party doubtful (PDF TYK-4 DGGI raid) → attend count directly or arrange another auditor + inspect challans/e-way bills

Litigation & claims — procedures

  • Design procedures to identify litigation & claims giving rise to RMM — inquiry of management (and in-house counsel), review of minutes of TCWG meetings, review of legal expense account
  • If assessed risk of material misstatement → seek direct communication with external legal counsel via a letter of inquiry prepared by management & sent by the auditor (PDF TYK-3 — auditor sending letter herself is incorrect)
  • Letter contents: list of litigation & claims, mgmt's assessment of outcome, financial implications, request for external counsel's confirmation of reasonableness
  • If unable to communicate with external legal counsel or counsel refuses → modify opinion per SA 705
  • Obtain Written Representation that all known actual or possible litigation & claims have been disclosed & appropriately accounted for

Segment information — procedures

  • Obtain understanding of methods used by management to determine segment info & whether likely to result in disclosure per FRF — matters: inter-segment sales & eliminations, comparison with budgets, allocation of assets & costs, consistency with prior periods & adequacy of disclosures on inconsistencies
  • Perform analytical procedures or other procedures appropriate

Exceptions / Conditions

"Selective examination of specific items" (large-value or unusual items picked at auditor's judgment — PDF opening vignette of CA Kartik) is not audit sampling — subject only to non-sampling risk & results cannot be projected to population.

Auditor's Responsibilities

  • Plan attendance considering nature, location, timing, IC over inventory
  • Identify litigation/claims proactively (not wait for mgmt to disclose)

Conclusion / Reporting

SAAE unobtainable on any of the three items → modify opinion per SA 705; WR on completeness of litigation/claims supports but does not replace other evidence.

SA 505 External Confirmations

Scope: Auditor's use of external confirmation procedures to obtain audit evidence in accordance with SA 500; assists in designing & performing confirmations to obtain relevant & reliable evidence. PDF status (§1.1a): Intermediate depth; Final application only.

Objective (from PDF — verbatim)

"To design and perform external confirmation procedures to obtain relevant and reliable audit evidence."

Key Definitions

  • Positive confirmation — reply required whether or not confirming party agrees
  • Negative confirmation — reply only if confirming party disagrees with the information
  • Non-response — confirming party does not reply, or reply is undeliverable
  • Exception — response indicates a difference between the info requested/supplied & the confirming party's records

Key Requirements

  • Maintain control over the confirmation process: determine info to be confirmed · select confirming party · design request · send (incl. follow-ups)
  • If management refuses the auditor to send a request: inquire reasons & seek evidence of their validity/reasonableness; evaluate implication on RMM (incl. fraud) & on N/T/E of other procedures; perform alternative procedures. If refusal unreasonable → communicate with TCWG & consider implication on opinion
  • If a response is unreliable → evaluate implications for RMM (incl. fraud) & N/T/E of other procedures. (PDF TYK-5 — GST registrations cancelled for 25 of 30 creditors → responses unreliable)
  • Non-response to a positive confirmation → perform alternative audit procedures; if response is necessary to obtain SAAE (and alternative procedures can't) → determine implications on opinion
  • Investigate exceptions to determine whether they indicate misstatement

Negative confirmations — restrictive use

Use negative confirmation as sole substantive procedure only if ALL: (a) assessed RMM is low and relevant controls operate effectively; (b) population is large, homogeneous, small balances; (c) very low exception rate expected; (d) auditor is not aware of circumstances that would cause recipients to disregard. Non-response to a negative confirmation provides significantly less persuasive evidence than a positive reply.

Reliability risk factors

  • Response received indirectly (not directly by the auditor) · appears not to come from the originally intended party · oral-only responses (document & follow up for written)
  • All responses carry some risk of interception, alteration, fraud — electronic or paper
  • Confirmation signed by an inappropriate person at the confirming party (PDF MCQ — "signed by plant manager" → not reliable)

Auditor's Responsibilities

  • Retain control over the confirmation process; evaluate reliability of responses
  • If unable to obtain sufficient & reliable response (incl. mgmt-refusal unreasonable) → communicate TCWG, consider opinion impact

Conclusion / Reporting

Non-response + alternative procedures fail, or reliability doubts unresolved → qualified / disclaimer per SA 705.

SA 510 Initial Audit Engagements — Opening Balances

Scope: Auditor's responsibilities for opening balances in an initial audit engagement — where prior-period FS were unaudited or audited by a predecessor. PDF status (§1.1a): Intermediate depth; Final application only.

Objective (from PDF — verbatim)

"To obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence about: 1. Whether opening balances contain misstatements that materially affect the current period's financial statements. 2. Whether appropriate accounting policies are consistently applied and any changes where to has been properly accounted for, presented and disclosed."

Key Definitions

  • Initial audit engagement — engagement where prior period was unaudited OR audited by a predecessor auditor
  • Opening balances — balances at the beginning of the period, based on prior-period closing balances & accounting policies applied then
  • Predecessor auditor — auditor from a different firm who audited the prior period

Key Requirements

  • Read the most recent FS & predecessor auditor's report for info relevant to opening balances
  • Determine whether prior-period closing balance has been correctly brought forward
  • Determine whether opening balances reflect appropriate accounting policies
  • Perform one or more of: (i) review predecessor's working papers, (ii) evaluate whether current-period procedures provide evidence, (iii) specific audit procedures on opening balances. For inventory — current period's closing count provides little evidence of opening inventory → observe a current count & reconcile; perform procedures on valuation of opening inventory, GP, cut-off (PDF TYK-6)
  • If current-period FS misstated due to misstatement in opening balances → communicate with mgmt/TCWG per SA 450/SA 250

Reporting matrix

ConditionOpinion
Unable to obtain SAAE re opening balancesQualified / Disclaimer
Opening balances contain misstatement materially affecting current period, not properly presented/disclosedQualified / Adverse
Accounting policies not consistently applied or change not properly accounted forQualified / Adverse
Predecessor's report was modified & modification remains relevant & material to current FSCurrent report modified accordingly

Auditor's Responsibilities

  • If prior-period unaudited → perform procedures directly on opening balances
  • If audited by predecessor → rely on predecessor where reliance is possible & appropriate, supplemented

Conclusion / Reporting

As per matrix above — opening-balance issues flow straight into SA 705 modifications; predecessor's modification may survive into current-period report.

SA 530 Audit Sampling

Scope: Use of statistical & non-statistical sampling in designing/selecting the sample, performing TOC & tests of details, and evaluating results. Applies when auditor has decided to use audit sampling. PDF status (§1.1a): Intermediate depth; Final application only.

Objective (from PDF — verbatim)

"To provide a reasonable basis for the auditor to draw conclusions about the population from which the sample is selected."

Key Definitions

  • Audit sampling — application of audit procedures to less than 100% of items in a population of audit relevance, such that all sampling units have a chance of selection, to provide the auditor with a reasonable basis on which to draw conclusions about the entire population
  • Sampling risk — auditor's sample-based conclusion may differ from conclusion if entire population were tested
  • Non-sampling risk — risk of erroneous conclusion for reasons unrelated to sampling (inappropriate procedures, misinterpreting evidence, failing to recognise a misstatement/deviation)
  • Anomaly — a misstatement/deviation demonstrably not representative of the population
  • Projected misstatement — auditor's best estimate, in populations of the misstatement, extrapolated from the sample

Key Requirements

  • Consider purpose of the procedure & characteristics of the population when designing the sample
  • Determine sample size sufficient to reduce sampling risk to an acceptably low level
  • Select items such that each sampling unit has a chance of selection (random · systematic · MUS · haphazard · block — block avoided unless justified)
  • Perform the procedure on each selected item; if procedure inapplicable → apply on a replacement item; if auditor cannot apply the procedure or suitable replacement → treat as a deviation / misstatement
  • Investigate nature & cause of deviations/misstatements; if treating as anomaly obtain high degree of certainty it is not representative
  • For tests of details: project misstatements to the population; evaluate whether sampling provided a reasonable basis for conclusions

Two species of sampling risk (PDF TYK-7)

Erroneous conclusionEffect
Controls less effective than they are · material misstatement exists when it doesn'tAffects audit efficiency — additional work establishes initial conclusion was wrong
Controls more effective than they are · material misstatement does not exist when it doesAffects audit effectiveness — more likely to lead to inappropriate opinion

If sampling did not provide a reasonable basis

  • Request management to investigate misstatements & the potential for further misstatements & make necessary adjustments, OR
  • Tailor N/T/E of further procedures — e.g., extend sample size, test an alternative control, modify related substantive procedures

Exceptions / Conditions

"Selective examination of specific items" (auditor judgement-picks of high-value or unusual items) is not audit sampling. Its results cannot be projected. Only exposed to non-sampling risk.

Conclusion / Reporting

Sample-based conclusions feed into SA 330 response adequacy; material misstatements uncovered flow into SA 450.

SA 540 Auditing Accounting Estimates & Related Disclosures (including Fair Value Estimates)

Scope: Auditor's responsibilities for accounting estimates (including fair-value estimates) & related disclosures.

SA 540 is deferred to Intermediate level per the ICAI Final SM — it is not reproduced in the Ch 5 PDF. Objective and detailed requirements are not reproduced in this chapter's PDF. Refer Intermediate SM for revision. No ICAI-verbatim text fabricated here.

Keywords to remember (refresher)

  • Inherent-risk factors: estimation uncertainty · complexity · subjectivity · other (including susceptibility to management bias)
  • Risk response: test how mgmt made the estimate (method, assumptions, data); develop auditor's point estimate or range; subsequent events review; auditor's expert (SA 620) for complex valuations
  • Disclosure evaluation — reasonableness of key assumptions, sensitivity, range of possible outcomes
  • Retrospective review of prior-period estimates for management bias
  • Reporting — material misstatement / biased assumptions → modify opinion (SA 705); Key Audit Matter (SA 701) where high estimation uncertainty

SA 550 Related Parties

Scope: Auditor's responsibilities regarding related party relationships & transactions. Expands how SA 315, SA 330, SA 240 are applied to RP risks. PDF status (§1.1a): Intermediate depth; Final application only.

Objective (from PDF — verbatim)

"I. To obtain an understanding of the related party transactions to: 1. recognize fraud risk factors arising from such transactions. 2. conclude whether financial statements in so far as they are affected by such transactions: a) achieve a true and fair presentation. b) are not misleading. II. To obtain audit evidence as to whether the related party transactions are properly identified, accounted for, and disclosed in the financial statements."

Key Definitions

  • Related party — per the applicable FRF (e.g., Ind AS 24 / AS 18 / Sec 2(76) Companies Act)
  • Arm's-length transaction — conducted on terms between willing parties other than in a RP relationship, behaving independently

Key Requirements — six-step workflow

  • Risk-assessment procedures & related activities
  • Understand the entity's RP relationships & transactions (inquire mgmt, review supporting docs)
  • Maintain alertness for RP info when reviewing records/documents
  • Share RP information with the engagement team
  • Identify & assess RMM associated with RP relationships & transactions
  • Design responses to assessed RMM

Previously unidentified / undisclosed RPs or transactions (PDF — heightened risk)

  • Determine whether underlying circumstances confirm existence; communicate promptly within team
  • Request mgmt to identify all transactions with the newly identified RP; inquire why controls failed
  • Perform additional substantive procedures; reconsider risk of other undisclosed RPs
  • If non-disclosure appears intentional → evaluate implications for audit; apply SA 240 fraud procedures; re-evaluate reliability of mgmt reps & prior responses
  • PDF Case-Study-2 (SRA JV): verify MCA website, MBP-1 data, past dealings to establish nexus (e.g., ₹20 crore paid to "Useless & Sons" vs ₹5 crore equity contribution of MD)

Significant RP transactions outside the normal course of business

  • Inspect underlying contracts & evaluate — (a) business rationale (and whether it suggests fraudulent financial reporting or concealment of misappropriation); (b) whether terms consistent with mgmt's explanations; (c) whether properly accounted for & disclosed
  • Obtain evidence that the transactions were appropriately authorised & approved
  • Evaluate rationale from the related party's perspective — absence of rationale = fraud risk factor (PDF TYK-8: sale of blanket machinery to co. that does not manufacture blankets)

Written Representations + TCWG communication

  • Obtain WR that: (a) all RPs & RP transactions have been disclosed to the auditor; (b) all such transactions have been properly accounted for & disclosed
  • Communicate significant matters regarding the entity's RPs to TCWG
  • Documentation: names of RPs & nature of RP transactions

Exceptions / Conditions

RP transactions can be more susceptible to management override; complex structures (SPEs, off-balance-sheet, unclear governance) heighten risk. Assertions that an RP transaction is at arm's length may be a rebuttable claim — obtain evidence; don't accept on face value.

Conclusion / Reporting

Material undisclosed RPs / non-arm's-length terms not properly disclosed → modify opinion per SA 705. Suspicion of fraud → consider withdrawal (where legally permitted) & duty to report per SA 240.

SA 560 Subsequent Events

Scope: Auditor's responsibilities relating to subsequent events in an FS audit.

SA 560 is deferred to Intermediate level per the ICAI Final SM — it is not reproduced in the Ch 5 PDF. Objective and detailed requirements are not reproduced in this chapter's PDF. Refer Intermediate SM for revision. No ICAI-verbatim text fabricated here. (Primary revisit point is Ch 6 Completion & Review per SPEC §11.)

Keywords to remember (refresher)

  • Two windows: date of FS → date of auditor's report (active procedures) · after auditor's report → date FS issued (no duty unless facts come to attention)
  • Type I (adjusting) — provide evidence of conditions existing at BS date → adjust FS
  • Type II (non-adjusting) — indicate conditions arising after BS date → disclose
  • Active procedures: inquiries, reading minutes, reviewing latest interim FS, obtaining WR
  • Facts known after report but before FS issued → discuss with mgmt, determine if FS need amendment; if mgmt declines → take action to prevent reliance on report
  • Facts known after FS issued → consider whether FS need revision; if revised, new audit report dated no earlier than revised-FS approval

SA 570 Going Concern

Scope: Auditor's responsibilities regarding management's use of the going concern basis of accounting & the conclusion on the appropriateness of that basis.

SA 570 is deferred to Intermediate level per the ICAI Final SM — it is not reproduced in the Ch 5 PDF. Objective and detailed requirements are not reproduced in this chapter's PDF. Refer Intermediate SM for revision. No ICAI-verbatim text fabricated here. The flowchart below is a whitelisted structural refresher (SPEC §9) — primary coverage for going-concern also recurs in Ch 6.

Keywords to remember (refresher)

  • Events or conditions — financial (net liabilities, loan default, adverse ratios), operating (loss of key market/customer, labour issues), other (legal/regulatory, uninsured catastrophe)
  • Management's assessment — at least 12 months from FS date; auditor evaluates same period
  • Material uncertainty (MUO) — may cast significant doubt on ability to continue as going concern
  • Four reporting outcomes (see flowchart) — appropriate / MUO-adequately-disclosed / MUO-inadequately-disclosed / assumption-inappropriate

SA 580 Written Representations

Scope: Obtaining Written Representations (WR) from management and, where appropriate, TCWG.

SA 580 is deferred to Intermediate level per the ICAI Final SM — it is not reproduced in the Ch 5 PDF. Objective and detailed requirements are not reproduced in this chapter's PDF. Refer Intermediate SM for revision. No ICAI-verbatim text fabricated here. (Primary revisit point is Ch 6 Completion & Review per SPEC §11.)

Keywords to remember (refresher)

  • Addressee — typically those with responsibility for FS & knowledge of the matters (e.g., CEO/CFO, Board)
  • Date — as near as practicable to, but not after, the date of the auditor's report; period — all periods covered
  • Mandatory WRs — fulfilment of mgmt's responsibilities for FS preparation, internal control, providing all information & access, recording of all transactions; plus WRs required by other SAs (e.g., SA 240 fraud, SA 250 NOCLAR, SA 450 uncorrected misstatements, SA 501 litigation, SA 540 estimates, SA 550 RPs, SA 560 subsequent events, SA 570 going concern)
  • Refusal / doubt on reliability / lack of integrity of mgmt → impact: evaluate effect on other reliance; re-evaluate integrity; if fundamental → disclaimer of opinion
  • WR supports — but does not substitute — other audit evidence

Relevance-vs-reliability — direction of testing quick-drill

  • Existence — start from the book, trace to the physical item: pick a line in the fixed-asset register → inspect the asset
  • Completeness — start from the source, trace into the book: pick a GRN/challan → trace to purchase ledger
  • Misaligning direction = procedure tests the wrong assertion — even with ample evidence, quality/relevance is zero
