Chapter map

The SA 800-series addresses specialised reporting engagements — where the financial information being audited is not a vanilla complete set of general-purpose financial statements. Three SAs, three distinct fact-patterns: SA 800 covers FS prepared under a special purpose framework; SA 805 covers a single FS or a specific element, account or item; SA 810 covers a report on summary financial statements derived from previously audited FS. Expect roughly 6–10 marks combined on this chapter, typically mixing MCQs on scope distinctions with a descriptive question on reporting wording or acceptance conditions.

Common thread: each SA does not override the other SAs — it layers on special considerations for acceptance, planning & performance, and opinion & reporting. SA 800 and SA 805 both hinge on framework acceptability and alerting readers; SA 810 hinges on derivation consistency with the already-audited FS.

SA cluster overview

SA 800 (Revised) Special Considerations — Audits of FS Prepared in Accordance with Special Purpose Frameworks

Scope: Special considerations for audits of FS prepared under a special purpose framework. Does not override other SAs.

Objective

"SA 800 addresses special considerations that are relevant to: (a) The acceptance of the engagement; (b) The planning and performance of that engagement; and (c) Forming an opinion and reporting on the financial statements."

Key Definitions

  • Special purpose framework — an FRF designed to meet the financial information needs of specific users; may be fair-presentation or compliance
  • General purpose framework — FRF designed to meet the common needs of a wide range of users
  • Examples of special purpose FRF: cash basis · regulator's reporting provisions · contract provisions (bond indenture, loan agreement, project grant)
  • "Financial statements" here means a complete set of special purpose FS including related notes (accounting policies + explanatory info)

Key Requirements

  • Acceptance: determine acceptability of the FRF by reference to the specific users' financial information needs; regulator-prescribed FRF is presumed acceptable absent contrary indication
  • Planning: comply with all SAs relevant to the audit; SA 320 materiality judged against intended users (not a wide group); obtain understanding of significant interpretations of a contract
  • A management-agreed threshold with intended users does not relieve the auditor from SA 320 materiality for planning/performance
  • Opinion & reporting: apply SA 700 (Revised); report shall describe the purpose of the FS and, if necessary, the intended users
  • Mandatory alert: include an Emphasis of Matter paragraph per SA 706 (Revised) stating FS are prepared under a special purpose framework and may not be suitable for another purpose
  • May restrict distribution or use of the report to the specific users; expand the alert paragraph and modify its heading accordingly

Exceptions / Conditions

Where FRF is supplemented by legislative/regulatory requirements → SA 210 requires the auditor to identify any conflicts and act as prescribed.
If contract-based FRF falls outside prescribed classes, acceptability is tested against the attributes normally exhibited by acceptable FRFs (SA 210); relative weight of each attribute is a matter of professional judgment.
Where a complete set of general purpose FS is also prepared, TCWG for SPF oversight may differ from TCWG for GPF oversight (SA 260 (Revised)).

Auditor's Responsibilities

  • Evaluate whether FS adequately describe the FRF and any significant contract interpretations
  • SA 701 KAM applies in its entirety if KAM is communicated
  • SA 720 (Revised) applies to any document accompanying SPF FS whose purpose is to inform owners / similar stakeholders — treat as an annual report
  • May refer in an Other Matter paragraph to the auditor's report on the complete GPF FS (e.g. a Material Uncertainty Related to Going Concern disclosed there)

Conclusion / Reporting

Unmodified opinion plus mandatory EoM alert. Example heading: "Emphasis of Matter — Basis of Accounting and Restriction on Distribution and Use". Opinion is not modified by the EoM itself.

SA 805 (Revised) Special Considerations — Audits of Single FS and Specific Elements, Accounts or Items of a FS

Scope: Special considerations when applying SAs 100-700 to an audit of a single FS or a specific element. Framework may be general or special purpose (if special, SA 800 also applies). Does not apply to a component auditor's report produced for a principal auditor in a consolidated-FS audit.

Objective

"The objective of the auditor, when applying SAs in an audit of a single financial statement or of a specific element, account or item of a financial statement, is to address appropriately the special considerations that are relevant to: (a) The acceptance of the engagement; (b) The planning and performance of that engagement; and (c) Forming an opinion and reporting on the single financial statement or on the specific element, account or item of a financial statement."

Key Definitions

  • Single financial statement — distinguished from a complete set; e.g. a cash flow statement alone
  • Element of a FS — an element, account or item; e.g. trade receivables, cash and bank balances, schedule of net tangible assets, schedule of disbursements under a lease
  • Each includes related notes (accounting policies + explanatory info relevant to the FS/element)

Key Requirements

  • Acceptance — practicability test: if auditor is not also engaged on the complete set, determine whether the audit of the single FS/element under SAs is practicable
  • Assess acceptability of the FRF — whether it produces adequate disclosures for intended users; for element-only engagements, confirm the derivative framework contains relevant requirements of the parent framework
  • Planning: adapt all relevant SAs; even for an element audit, SA 240, SA 550, SA 570 (Revised) remain relevant in principle (fraud, related parties, going concern)
  • Materiality for the single FS / element may be lower than for the complete set — affects N/T/E of procedures and evaluation of uncorrected misstatements
  • Form of opinion — consider whether "presents fairly / true and fair" is appropriate; for compliance framework, use "prepared, in all material respects, in accordance with…"
  • Separate opinion when done in conjunction with the audit of the complete set; differentiate presentation if published together

Exceptions / Conditions

Disproportionate effort: some SAs (e.g. SA 570 on going concern for a receivables schedule) may be impracticable — discuss with management whether another type of engagement would be more practicable.
Adverse / Disclaimer on the full FS → cannot issue an unmodified opinion on a single FS from that set in the same report (SA 705 prohibits contradiction). For a specific element, unmodified opinion is permitted only if (a) not prohibited by law, (b) published in a separate report, and (c) the element is not a major portion of the FS.
An unmodified opinion on a single FS of the complete set is never permitted where the overall opinion is adverse/disclaimed — a single FS is deemed to constitute a major portion of the FS.
Disclaimer on results/cash flows + unmodified on state of affairs is permitted in the same report on the complete set (the disclaimer is not on the FS as a whole).

Auditor's Responsibilities

  • Adapt all relevant SAs to the circumstances; perform procedures on interrelated items to meet the audit objective
  • Consider implications of modifications / EoM / OM / MURGC / uncorrected-other-information statements in the report on the complete FS for the single-FS / element engagement
  • Sign in personal name + firm name (if firm appointed) + ICAI membership no. + FRN; state UDIN

Conclusion / Reporting

Separate opinion per engagement. If published together with the complete set, differentiate the opinion or insist management rectify the presentation before issuing the report.

SA 810 (Revised) Engagements to Report on Summary Financial Statements

Scope: Auditor's responsibilities when engaged to report on summary FS derived from FS audited by that same auditor under SAs.

Objective

"SA 810 deals with the auditor's responsibilities when undertaking an engagement to report on summary financial statements derived from financial statements audited in accordance with SAs by that same auditor."

Key Definitions

  • Summary FS — historical financial information derived from FS; less detail, but a structured representation consistent with the source FS of the entity's resources/obligations at a point in time or changes over a period
  • Applied criteria — criteria management applies to prepare the summary FS

Key Requirements — Acceptance

  • Same-auditor rule — ordinarily accept only if the auditor audited the source FS under SAs (else no knowledge base to discharge SA 810 responsibilities)
  • Determine applied criteria are acceptable — factors: nature of entity, purpose, intended users' needs, whether criteria avoid being misleading
  • Where criteria are set by recognised body or law/regulation → presume acceptable (per SA 210 analogy)
  • Obtain management's written agreement that it is responsible for: preparing summary FS per criteria · making audited FS available to intended users · including the auditor's report in any document presenting the summary FS
  • Agree on the form of opinion with management

Key Requirements — Procedures

  • Evaluate whether summary FS disclose their summarised nature and identify the audited FS
  • If summary FS not accompanied by audited FS → check that they describe from whom/where the audited FS are available (or cite the law that says they need not be)
  • Evaluate adequate disclosure of applied criteria
  • Compare / re-calculate the summary FS against the related information in the audited FS
  • Evaluate preparation per applied criteria; aggregation level; not misleading in the circumstances
  • Evaluate availability of the audited FS to intended users without undue difficulty

Key Requirements — Form of Opinion

  • Unmodified opinion uses one of: "consistent, in all material respects, with the audited FS, in accordance with the applied criteria" OR "a fair summary of the audited FS, in accordance with the applied criteria"
  • If law prescribes different wording: apply procedures + evaluate whether users might misunderstand; if additional explanation cannot mitigate misunderstanding → do not accept (report, if issued under law, cannot indicate compliance with SA 810)

Elements of the Report (SA 810)

  • Title indicating independent auditor · addressee · identification of summary FS and of the audited FS
  • Clear expression of opinion · statement that summary FS omit disclosures required by the source FRF and reading them is not a substitute for reading the audited FS
  • Reference to the auditor's report on the audited FS, its date, and the fact that an unmodified opinion was expressed on the audited FS
  • Mgmt's responsibility for summary FS · auditor's responsibility to express an opinion under SA 810
  • Signature (personal + firm), FRN, ICAI membership no., UDIN, date, place
  • Date of the report on summary FS — no earlier than: (i) date SAAE obtained + responsibility asserted by recognised authority; AND (ii) date of report on audited FS

Exceptions / Conditions — Linkage to Audited FS Report

Audited FS carries qualified opinion / EoM / OM / MURGC / KAM / uncorrected-other-information statement → if summary FS are still consistent/fair summary, report on summary FS must state that fact AND describe the basis and effect on the summary FS.
Audited FS carries adverse opinion or disclaimer → report on summary FS must (a) state that fact, (b) describe the basis, and (c) state that as a result it is inappropriate to express an opinion on the summary FS.
Summary FS not consistent / not fair summary and mgmt refuses to correct → express adverse opinion on the summary FS.
Distribution/use restriction or SPF alert on audited FS report → mirror that restriction/alert in the summary-FS report.

Auditor's Responsibilities — Subsequent Events & Association

  • If report on summary FS is dated later than report on audited FS → state that neither reflects post-report events
  • If auditor becomes aware of a fact existing at the date of the audited-FS report → apply SA 560 before issuing the summary-FS report
  • Unaudited supplementary information presented with summary FS → demand clear differentiation; if refused, state in report that such info is not covered
  • Read information in the document containing summary FS for material inconsistency; if uncorrected, consider implications for the report
  • Auditor association: if entity refers to auditor's name in a document with summary FS but omits the auditor's report — demand inclusion; if refused, take appropriate action to prevent inappropriate association
  • Comparatives: if audited FS contain comparatives but summary FS do not — determine reasonableness; if summary FS carry comparatives reported by another auditor, apply SA 710 inclusions

Conclusion / Reporting

Express opinion using "consistent" or "fair summary" language, linked to the audited FS and the applied criteria; modify per the linkage rules above.

Keywords to remember (revision only)

  • SA 600 (Revised): principal auditor · component auditor · materiality to CFS · division of responsibility · no review duty of component workpapers unless needed
  • SA 610: internal audit function · objectivity & competence · nature & extent of reliance · direct assistance (where permitted) · external auditor's sole responsibility for opinion
  • SA 620: auditor's expert (internal vs external) · competence, capabilities, objectivity · agreement on scope · evaluation of adequacy of work · reference in auditor's report only where required by law/regulation

Engagement-acceptance decision — Ch 8 fact-patterns

5 Exam Triggers — spot these phrasings, invoke these SAs

  1. "FS prepared per financial reporting provisions of a loan agreement (or bond indenture) between the client and a specific lender" → SA 800: contract-based SPF; unmodified opinion + mandatory Emphasis of Matter alert (basis of accounting); consider restricting distribution to the specific users.
  2. "Auditor has expressed an adverse opinion on the company's complete set of FS; now offered a separate engagement to audit the cash flow statement alone" → SA 805: cannot issue an unmodified opinion on a single FS of that set (single FS = major portion); unmodified on a specific element may be permitted only if published separately + element is not a major portion + not prohibited by law.
  3. "CA G offered audit of trade payables only; complete FS audited by another auditor (CA Jignesh)" → SA 805: practicability problem — no understanding of entity/IC, no base audit evidence; consider another engagement type; materiality likely lower; SA 240/550/570 still relevant in principle.
  4. "Mutual Fund trustees prepare abridged FS per SEBI format; audited FS already audited with unmodified opinion by the same auditor" → SA 810: applied criteria (SEBI format) presumed acceptable; opinion as "fair summary of audited FS, in accordance with applied criteria"; report states abridged FS are not a substitute for audited FS and references date & unmodified opinion of the audited-FS report.
  5. "Audited FS carry a qualified opinion on allowance for doubtful debts; client now wants a report on summary FS that still reflects a fair summary" → SA 810: report on summary FS shall state the audited-FS report contains a qualified opinion, describe the basis, and the effect (if any) on the summary FS; cannot upgrade to an unmodified conclusion on the summary set. If audited-FS report were adverse/disclaimer → must state it is inappropriate to express an opinion on the summary FS.
