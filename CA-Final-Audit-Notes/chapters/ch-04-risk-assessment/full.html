Chapter map

This chapter wires together the risk-response pipeline of every audit. SA 315 identifies & assesses risks of material misstatement (RMM) at the F/S level and at the assertion level, grounded in understanding the entity & its internal control. SA 320 sets the yardstick — materiality for the FS as a whole, performance materiality, specific materiality, clearly trivial. SA 330 converts assessed risks into responses — overall (at F/S level) plus further procedures (tests of controls + substantive) at the assertion level. SA 450 closes the loop — accumulate misstatements, classify, request correction, reassess materiality, evaluate effect on opinion, obtain written reps.

The risk-response pipeline (opening cluster)

Foundation — Audit risk model & assertions (from PDF)

Audit risk is the risk of expressing an inappropriate audit opinion on FS that are materially misstated. Two components: Risk of Material Misstatement (IR × CR — exists before the audit starts) and Detection Risk (auditor's failure to detect despite procedures). Combined IR+CR is inversely related to DR, and materiality is inversely related to audit risk.

ComponentCoreLevel addressed
Inherent RiskSusceptibility of an assertion to material misstatement assuming no related controlsF/S level + assertion level
Control RiskRisk that entity's IC will not prevent or timely detect & correct a material misstatementPrimarily assertion level (entity-level pervasive)
Detection RiskRisk that auditor will not detect an existing misstatementAssertion level — inverse to assessed RMM

Assertions tested (SA 315 taxonomy): CoT & events — occurrence, completeness, accuracy, cut-off, classification. Account balances — existence, rights & obligations, completeness, valuation & allocation. Presentation & disclosure — occurrence & R&O, completeness, classification & understandability, accuracy & valuation.

SA 315 Identifying and Assessing the Risks of Material Misstatement Through Understanding the Entity and Its Environment

Scope: Auditor's responsibility to identify & assess RMM at the F/S level and assertion level via understanding the entity, its environment, and its system of internal control. PDF status (§1.1a): deferred to Intermediate; at Final only application/case-study is expected.

Objective

ICAI-verbatim Objective sentence for SA 315 is not reproduced in the Ch 4 Final SM PDF (expressly deferred to Intermediate, per note on p. 4.72 of the PDF). Not fabricated here — refer Intermediate SM for the full Objective clause.

Key Definition (ICAI verbatim — from PDF)

"SA 315 defines the system of internal control as the process designed, implemented and maintained by those charged with governance, management and other personnel to provide reasonable assurance about the achievement of an entity's objectives with regard to reliability of financial reporting, effectiveness and efficiency of operations, safeguarding of assets, and compliance with applicable laws and regulations."

Key Definitions (revision)

  • Risk of material misstatement (RMM) = Inherent Risk × Control Risk — the risk the FS are misstated prior to audit
  • Significant risk — an identified & assessed RMM that requires special audit consideration (presumption: revenue recognition fraud, management override)
  • Assertion-level risk vs F/S-level risk — the foundational split of SA 315
  • Business risk — resulting from significant conditions/events that adversely affect the entity's objectives; most business risks eventually have FR consequences

F/S-level RMM vs Assertion-level RMM (the distinction SA 315 drives)

DimensionF/S levelAssertion level
NaturePervasive to FS as a whole — affects many assertionsSpecific to a CoT, balance or disclosure's assertion (C/E/A/V/R&O/Cut-off)
PDF exampleIncompetent senior accountant → errors spread across balances & assertionsObsolescence in inventory → valuation assertion high; in payables low
Response (→ SA 330)Overall responses — skepticism emphasis, more experienced staff, added supervision, unpredictability, change in N/T/EFurther audit procedures — tests of controls + substantive (tests of details + analytical)

Key Requirements (auditor's risk-assessment workflow)

  • Perform risk assessment procedures — inquiries (of management, TCWG, internal audit, others), analytical procedures, observation & inspection
  • Understand the entity & environment — industry/regulatory/external factors, nature of entity, accounting policies, objectives & strategies, measurement & review of FP
  • Understand the system of internal control — five components: control environment · entity's risk assessment process · control activities · information system & communication · monitoring
  • Evaluate design & implementation of relevant controls (whether capable of preventing, or detecting-and-correcting, material misstatements)
  • Identify risks throughout the process of obtaining understanding & relate each to what can go wrong at the assertion level
  • Determine whether any identified risk is a significant risk requiring special audit consideration (and test those controls at least once in the period if reliance placed)

Entity-level components of IC (COSO-aligned, per PDF)

  • Control environment — integrity & ethics, commitment to competence, TCWG participation, management philosophy, org structure, authority/responsibility, HR policies (tone at the top)
  • Entity's risk assessment process — how management identifies, estimates significance & likelihood, and responds to business risks
  • Control activities — performance reviews, information processing (IT general + application controls), physical controls, segregation of duties
  • Information system & communication — initiate, record, process, report transactions; policy/procedure manuals, memoranda, electronic
  • Monitoring of controls — ongoing activities, separate evaluations, internal audit, external communications (regulators, customers)

Exceptions / Conditions

Risk assessment is iterative & dynamic — as further procedures yield information, original assessments may need revision (e.g., control deviations discovered during TOC may raise CR and force modification of N/T/E of substantive procedures). Per PDF: development of overall audit plan does not require an understanding of control procedures for every assertion in each balance.
Inherent limitations of any IC: cost-benefit constraints, human error, management override, collusion, controls not directed at unusual transactions. Hence reasonable — not absolute — assurance.

Auditor's Responsibilities

  • Document: discussions among engagement team, understanding of entity & IC, risks identified at both levels, significant risks & related controls
  • Communicate to TCWG & management — significant deficiencies in IC identified (SA 265 hand-off)
  • Revise risk assessment if new information indicates assessed level is inappropriate

Conclusion / Reporting

Identified & assessed RMMs — at F/S level and at assertion level, with significant risks flagged — feed directly into SA 330 responses. No separate report output from SA 315 itself.

SA 320 Materiality in Planning and Performing an Audit

Scope: Auditor's responsibility to apply the concept of materiality when planning, performing & evaluating. PDF status (§1.1a): deferred to Intermediate; at Final only application/case-study is expected. The PDF's Q13, Q14 answers and Q1 set out the operational mechanics reproduced below.

Objective

ICAI-verbatim Objective sentence for SA 320 is not reproduced in the Ch 4 Final SM PDF (expressly deferred to Intermediate). Not fabricated here — refer Intermediate SM for the exact Objective clause.

Key Definitions (revision from PDF's application sections)

  • Materiality (FS as a whole) — threshold above which misstatements could reasonably be expected to influence economic decisions of users
  • Performance materiality — lower amount(s) set by the auditor to reduce to an appropriately low level the probability that the aggregate of uncorrected + undetected misstatements exceeds materiality for FS as a whole
  • Specific materiality — separate lower level(s) for particular classes of transactions, account balances or disclosures where misstatements of lesser amounts could influence users
  • Clearly trivial threshold — amount below which misstatements need not be accumulated (well below FS materiality; any doubt → not clearly trivial)

Four levels of materiality (the SA 320 hierarchy)

LevelPurposeWhere used
FS as a wholeOverall threshold for the auditPlanning, forming opinion
Specific (CoT/balance/disclosure)Sensitive items — related-party txns, mgmt remuneration, covenants, segment disclosuresPlanning & evaluation for those items
Performance materialityBuffer below FS materiality for planning & performingSample sizes, scope of substantive
Clearly trivialDe-minimis accumulation cut-offSA 450 accumulation decision

Benchmark selection factors (Q1 of PDF)

  • Elements of FS (assets, liabilities, equity, revenue, expenses)
  • Items on which users focus (profit, revenue, net assets)
  • Nature of entity · lifecycle stage · industry & economic environment
  • Ownership structure & financing (debt-financed → asset-focused users)
  • Relative volatility of the benchmark
Application tip (PDF): profitable mfr → PBT / Earnings. Loss-making infra with erratic profits → Revenue or Gross profit. Public-utility/NFP or custodians → Total cost / net cost / assets.

Key Requirements

  • Determine materiality for FS as a whole when establishing overall audit strategy
  • Determine specific materiality levels where misstatements of lesser amount could influence users
  • Determine performance materiality for planning & performing
  • Revise materiality if new information would have led to a different initial amount; if revised lower, reconsider performance materiality & whether N/T/E of further procedures remain appropriate
  • Document all four amounts + any subsequent revisions with reasons

Exceptions / Conditions

Materiality involves quantitative & qualitative considerations — a small amount affecting a debt-covenant breach, regulatory threshold, or trend can be material irrespective of size.
Where actual results are materially different from planning estimates (e.g., Q13 PDF case — construction co., last-month revenue 30% of estimate) → revise materiality and reconsider performance materiality before evaluating uncorrected misstatements under SA 450.

Auditor's Responsibilities

  • Apply materiality throughout — plan, perform, evaluate misstatements, form opinion
  • Coordinate with SA 315 (RMM assessment) and SA 330 (sample sizes ↔ performance materiality)

Conclusion / Reporting

Material uncorrected misstatements at the close of the audit → drive opinion modification under SA 705; re-feed into SA 450 evaluation.

SA 330 The Auditor's Responses to Assessed Risks

Scope: Auditor's responsibility to design & implement responses to the RMM identified & assessed under SA 315. The PDF devotes §7 of the chapter to SA 330 and quotes its text directly.

Objective (ICAI verbatim — from PDF)

"The objective of the auditor in accordance with SA 330 is to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence about the assessed risks of material misstatement, through designing and implementing appropriate responses to those risks."

Key Definitions

  • Overall response — addresses RMM at the F/S level: emphasise skepticism, assign more experienced staff, use experts, add supervision, incorporate unpredictability, change N/T/E
  • Further audit procedures — addresses RMM at the assertion level: tests of controls + substantive procedures (tests of details + substantive analytical)
  • Test of controls (TOC) — tests the operating effectiveness of relevant controls
  • Substantive procedures — designed to detect material misstatements at the assertion level

Key Requirements (two-level response, per PDF §7)

  • At F/S level: design & implement overall responses to assessed RMM
  • At assertion level: design & perform further procedures — N/T/E responsive to assessed RMM, considering reasons for assessment (IR + CR) and whether the auditor intends to rely on controls
  • Obtain more persuasive evidence the higher the assessed risk
  • Design & perform TOC when (a) assessment includes an expectation controls operate effectively (reliance), or (b) substantive procedures alone cannot provide SAAE (e.g., highly automated processing with no manual audit trail)
  • Obtain more persuasive evidence the greater the reliance placed on a control
  • Irrespective of assessed RMM, perform substantive procedures for each material class of transactions, account balance & disclosure (minimum substantive floor)

Assertion-level mix (the PDF's planning considerations)

  • Assertions that cannot be addressed by substantive alone — highly automated processing → rely on controls
  • Where testing controls would reduce the need/scope of substantive → TOC leveraged
  • Potential for substantive analytical procedures to reduce other procedures
  • Build in an element of unpredictability
  • Address management override of controls (SA 240 overlap) and other fraud scenarios
  • Specific procedures for identified significant risks

Exceptions / Conditions

If results of substantive procedures contradict the preliminary CR assessment → make specific inquiries; if supported, revise CR upwards and modify N/T/E of further substantive procedures accordingly.
Evidence about the operating effectiveness obtained by observation is point-in-time only — supplement with other TOC covering the period of intended reliance.

Auditor's Responsibilities

  • Evaluate whether assessed RMMs are appropriately responded to; if evidence is insufficient, perform additional procedures
  • Communicate significant deficiencies surfaced during responses to TCWG/management (link to SA 265)
  • Document overall responses, N/T/E of further procedures, linkage to assessed risks, conclusions

Conclusion / Reporting

Feeds into SA 450 evaluation and ultimately into the auditor's opinion — modification under SA 705 if SAAE not obtained or misstatements material.

SA 315 → SA 330 flowchart (risk ID to tailored response)

flowchart TD A[Understand entity + IC · SA 315] --> B[Identify risks at F/S level & assertion level] B --> C{Significant risk?} C -->|Yes| D[Special consideration: test controls at least once · designed substantive procedures] C -->|No| E[Standard assessment] D --> F{Intend to rely on controls OR substantive alone insufficient?} E --> F F -->|Yes| G[Tests of Controls + residual Substantive] F -->|No| H[Substantive-only approach] G --> I[Mandatory substantive for each material CoT / balance / disclosure] H --> I I --> J[Conclude N/T/E sufficient → to SA 450]

SA 450 Evaluation of Misstatements Identified during the Audit

Scope: Auditor's responsibility to evaluate the effect of identified misstatements on the audit and of uncorrected misstatements, if any, on the FS. Cited in the PDF at Q13 answer.

Objective

ICAI-verbatim Objective sentence of SA 450 is not reproduced in the Ch 4 Final SM PDF (only the title & operational requirement are cited at Q13). Not fabricated here. Per the PDF's own words (Q13 answer): "the auditor is required to reassess materiality, in accordance with SA 320 … before evaluating the impact of uncorrected misstatements."

Key Definitions

  • Misstatement — difference between reported amount/classification/presentation/disclosure and what is required under the FRF
  • Uncorrected misstatements — those accumulated during the audit that management has not corrected
  • Factual misstatement — about which there is no doubt (e.g., a mis-posted entry)
  • Judgmental misstatement — arising from unreasonable accounting estimates or inappropriate accounting policy selection/application
  • Projected misstatement — auditor's best estimate of misstatement in populations, extrapolated from samples
  • Clearly trivial — amount below which misstatements need not be accumulated (not merely small — well below SA 320 materiality)

Key Requirements

  • Accumulate all identified misstatements other than those clearly trivial
  • Communicate on a timely basis all accumulated misstatements to the appropriate level of management and request correction
  • Reassess whether the overall audit strategy and plan need revision if (a) nature of misstatements & circumstances suggest others may exist that, combined, could be material, or (b) aggregate approaches materiality
  • Before evaluating effect of uncorrected misstatements: reassess materiality under SA 320 — if revised lower, reconsider performance materiality & N/T/E of further procedures
  • Determine whether uncorrected misstatements are material — individually or in aggregate — considering size, nature & particular circumstances (qualitative)
  • Communicate uncorrected misstatements & their effect to TCWG, request correction; obtain written representation from management & TCWG that they believe the effects of uncorrected misstatements are immaterial (list attached)

Classification that drives action

TypeSourceAction
FactualSpecific, verifiable errorCorrected 1:1 — quantum certain
JudgmentalUnreasonable estimate / inappropriate policyDiscuss with mgmt; if not corrected → carry to aggregation
ProjectedSample extrapolation across populationInvestigate causes; extend testing if out of expectation

Exceptions / Conditions

Even if total uncorrected misstatements are below FS materiality, qualitative factors (e.g., affect covenant compliance, reverse a loss to a profit, change earnings-per-share trend, management compensation trigger, regulatory threshold) may make them material.
If management refuses to correct some/all uncorrected misstatements → understand reasons & consider implications for the opinion; if uncorrected misstatements remain material → modification under SA 705 (qualified / adverse).

Auditor's Responsibilities — written representations

  • Obtain WR from management & TCWG confirming: uncorrected misstatements (listed in an attachment) are immaterial individually & in aggregate
  • If WR refused / qualified → consider impact on evidence & opinion (link to SA 580)
  • Document: clearly trivial threshold, all accumulated misstatements, conclusion on uncorrected (material or not) with basis, written representation obtained

Conclusion / Reporting

Uncorrected misstatements material individually/aggregated → qualified / adverse opinion per SA 705. If evidence inadequate → qualified/disclaimer. Communicate to TCWG.

SA 450 flowchart — accumulate → classify → reassess → opinion

flowchart TD A[Identify misstatements during audit] --> B{Clearly trivial?} B -->|Yes| C[Ignore - no accumulation] B -->|No| D[Accumulate · classify: Factual / Judgmental / Projected] D --> E[Communicate to management · request correction] E --> F{Corrected?} F -->|Yes| G[Remove from schedule] F -->|No| H[Reassess SA 320 materiality - revise performance materiality if lower] H --> I{Uncorrected material individually or aggregated - qualitative + quantitative?} I -->|No| J[Communicate to TCWG · obtain WR on immateriality] I -->|Yes| K[Modify opinion · SA 705 qualified or adverse] J --> L[Unmodified opinion - document] K --> L

Internal control — auditor's evaluation (bridge from SA 315 into the audit plan)

The PDF (§3–§6) develops the IC angle at length. Revision essentials:

  • Five components (above). Objective of IC: reliability of FR · effectiveness & efficiency of operations · safeguarding of assets · compliance with laws & regulations.
  • Inherent limitations of IC: cost-benefit, human error, management override, collusion, unusual transactions outside routine, manipulation of estimates. Hence only reasonable assurance.
  • Structure of IC: segregation of duties (authorisation · execution · custody · records), authorisation of transactions, adequacy of records & documents, accountability & safeguarding of assets, independent checks. Apart from SoD — periodic rotation of duties, annual leave, custody-vs-books separation, mechanical cash devices, budgetary control.
  • Techniques of evaluating IC: Internal Control Questionnaire · Check List · Flow chart. ICQ is answered by client executives; Check List by the auditor/staff. ICQ Yes/No → weakness; Check List prompts narrative where a weakness may be material.
  • Letter of Weakness (Management Letter): lists weaknesses + suggested improvements; states scope not designed to determine IC adequacy; serves as reference for mgmt revision; may minimise legal liability (Re S.P. Catterson & Ltd. defence). Does not absolve the auditor from qualifying opinion where uncorrected defects are material.
  • Global frameworks (coverage-only): COSO (5 components, 17 principles, 3 objective categories) · CoCo (Canadian — Purpose, Commitment, Capability, Monitoring & Learning; 20 criteria) · COBIT (ISACA IT governance; 4 domains: PO, AI, DS, ME) · Turnbull (UK directors' guidance) · SOX §404 (management ICFR assessment + auditor opinion on ICFR & FS — integrated audit; PCAOB oversight).
  • Risk-Based Audit: three phases — Risk Assessment · Risk Response · Reporting — collapses directly onto SA 315 → SA 330 → SA 450 pipeline.

5 Exam Triggers — spot these phrasings, invoke these SAs

  1. "Auditor of a PSU insurance divisional office finds surveyors appointed by Divisional Manager for > ₹30 lakh claims when SOP mandates Divisional Claims Committee approval — had initially assessed RMM as low" → SA 315: controls not operating as laid down → revise control risk upwards → revise assessed RMM → under SA 330 modify N/T/E of substantive procedures (Test Your Understanding 1 of PDF).
  2. "Newly-appointed auditor of an NGO in the political/social field, first-year, FCRA cancellable, crowd-funded, prior year unaudited, foreign funds received" → SA 315: high inherent risk (nature of activity, foreign funds, lack of integrity benchmarks), high control risk (no formal controls given prior small scale), high detection risk (first-year, unfamiliar procedures) — respond per SA 330 with more persuasive evidence & experienced team (Test Your Understanding 2).
  3. "Construction company extrapolated 11-month sales to estimate full year; last-month actual only 30% of estimate; auditor had set materiality on estimated revenue" → SA 450 requires reassessment of materiality under SA 320 before evaluating uncorrected misstatements; if revised lower, reconsider performance materiality & N/T/E of further procedures (Q13 of PDF).
  4. "Listed-co auditor suspects company's IC may fail to capture insurance premiums falling due on company assets" → significant deficiency under SA 315/SA 265 (susceptibility of asset to loss is a deficiency factor even absent actual misstatement); communicate in writing to TCWG with description & potential effects (Test Your Understanding 5).
  5. "Head of procurement has complete control over purchasing + goods receipt + supplier payment approval with no review; staff not rotated for 5 yrs; store manager both holds inventory and maintains records" → SA 315 control-environment / control-activities deficiencies: breach of segregation of duties, no job rotation, custodian-with-records → high control risk; communicate to TCWG; under SA 330 respond with heavier substantive testing on purchases/payables/inventory (Q15 of PDF).
