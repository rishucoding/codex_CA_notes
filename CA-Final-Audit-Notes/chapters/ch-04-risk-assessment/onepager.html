Must-remember keywords

  • SA 315: RMM = IR × CR · F/S vs assertion · significant risks · 5 IC components · D&I
  • SA 320: FS-whole · performance · specific · clearly trivial · benchmark (PBT/revenue/assets) · revise
  • SA 330: overall response · further procedures · TOC when relying / substantive-alone insufficient · persuasive when risk higher · substantive floor
  • SA 450: accumulate · factual/judgmental/projected · reassess SA 320 · qualitative · WR on immateriality · SA 705 if uncorrected

5 Exam Triggers

  1. PSU insurance — surveyor approvals bypass DCC → SA 315 revise CR & RMM → SA 330 revise N/T/E.
  2. First-year NGO audit, FCRA, foreign funds, crowd-funded, political field → SA 315 high IR + CR + DR; SA 330 more persuasive evidence.
  3. 11-month extrapolation; last-month actual 30% of estimate → SA 450 reassess SA 320 materiality + performance materiality.
  4. IC may fail to capture insurance premiums becoming due on company assets → significant deficiency; SA 265 written comm to TCWG.
  5. Head of procurement controls buy-receive-pay with no review; no job rotation; custodian maintains records → SA 315 SoD breach → heavier substantive per SA 330.

Reporting bottom-lines

  • SA 315: feeds RMM (F/S + assertion) into SA 330; no separate report.
  • SA 320: drives scope & samples; revise → reconsider performance materiality & procedures.
  • SA 330: SAAE on assessed risks; substantive floor per material item; TOC when relying / substantive-alone inadequate.
  • SA 450: uncorrected material (individually/aggregated) → SA 705 qualified/adverse; WR from mgmt & TCWG.
