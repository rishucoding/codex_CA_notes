Ch 19 · One-pager — Professional Ethics & Liabilities

CA Act 1949 Schedules · Council Guidelines 2008 · Code of Ethics · ~20 marks

5 Fundamental Principles (Code of Ethics)

111 Integrity · 112 Objectivity · 113 Professional Competence & Due Care · 114 Confidentiality · 115 Professional Behaviour. Breach is routed through the CA Act Schedules. Threats (Self-interest · Self-review · Advocacy · Familiarity · Intimidation) are evaluated via the reasonable-and-informed-third-party test, then addressed by eliminating, applying safeguards, or declining/ending the engagement.

NOCLAR · Discipline · Liability

  • NOCLAR s.260 — senior PAs (KMP) of listed entities; s.360 — listed entities with net worth ≥ ₹250 cr.
  • BoD (FS · ₹1L / remove ≤ 3 mo) · DC (SS · ₹5L / remove any period) · Appeal within 90 days.
  • Cos Act s.147 — ₹25K–₹5L or 4× remn; willful → 1 yr + ₹50K–₹25L / 8×. s.143(12) fraud ≥ ₹1 cr → CG in 60 days. NFRA s.132(4) debar 6 mo–10 yrs.
  • CA Act s.24 falsely claiming member — ₹1K first / ₹5K or 6 mo subsequent.

5 exam triggers

  1. Contingent / % / commission fee → FS-I·10.
  2. Tax audits > 60/partner → Ch VI + SS-II·1.
  3. Social-media / newspaper advertising → FS-I·6 + FS-I·7.
  4. No writing to previous auditor → FS-I·8.
  5. Indebted > ₹1 L / non-audit fee > audit fee → Ch X / Ch IX.

Bottom line: cite the clause, name the forum (BoD/DC), state the penalty band, apply the exception test.
