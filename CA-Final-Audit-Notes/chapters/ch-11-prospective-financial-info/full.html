Chapter 11 — Prospective Financial Information & Other Assurance Services

ICAI SM Final P3 May 2026 · ~8–10 marks Covers SAE 3400 · SAE 3402 · SAE 3420 (SAE 3410 flagged, not in PDF)

PDF scope note. The Ch 11 Final SM PDF covers SAE 3400, SAE 3402 and SAE 3420 only. SAE 3410 (Assurance Engagements on Greenhouse Gas Statements) is not reproduced anywhere in this chapter's PDF — it is flagged below as out-of-PDF-scope and no ICAI-verbatim content is fabricated for it.

Chapter map

Assurance engagements that deal with subject matter other than historical financial statements — i.e. future-oriented projections, controls at outsourced service organisations, and hypothetical pro-forma illustrations in a prospectus. All three SAEs yield reasonable assurance on criteria-based subject matter, not an opinion on FS fairness.

SAE 3400 — examine forecasts / projections. SAE 3402 — service-auditor reports (Type 1 vs Type 2) relied on by user auditors under SA 402. SAE 3420 — report on compilation of pro-forma FI in a prospectus, testing compilation, not actual outcome.

SAE 3400 The Examination of Prospective Financial Information

Scope: Engagements to examine and report on prospective financial information (PFI) including procedures for best-estimate and hypothetical assumptions. Does not apply to PFI expressed in general / narrative terms (e.g. MD&A in annual report), though procedures may be suitable. "Auditor" here ≠ statutory auditor — examination need not be by the statutory auditor of the entity's FS.

Objective — verbatim

"In an engagement to examine prospective financial information, the auditor should obtain sufficient appropriate evidence as to whether: (a) management's best-estimate assumptions on which the prospective financial information is based are not unreasonable and, in the case of hypothetical assumptions, such assumptions are consistent with the purpose of the information; (b) the prospective financial information is properly prepared on the basis of the assumptions; (c) the prospective financial information is properly presented and all material assumptions are adequately disclosed, including a clear indication as to whether they are best-estimate assumptions or hypothetical assumptions; and (d) the prospective financial information is prepared on a consistent basis with historical financial statements, using appropriate accounting principles."

Key Definitions

  • Prospective financial information — FI based on assumptions about future events & possible actions; highly subjective, considerable judgment
  • Forecast — PFI on best-estimate assumptions (anticipated experience, no provision for risk of adverse deviation)
  • Projection — PFI on hypothetical assumptions OR mix of best-estimate + hypothetical (start-up, major change, imagined)
  • Best-estimate assumption — reflects anticipated experience, no provision for risk of adverse deviation

Acceptance considerations (pre-engagement)

  • Intended use of the information
  • General or limited distribution
  • Nature of assumptions — best-estimate vs hypothetical
  • Elements to be included
  • Period covered by the information
  • Extent to which reliance on entity's historical FI is justified
  • Agree terms via engagement letter

Examination procedures — N/T/E factors

  • Knowledge from previous engagements · management's competence to prepare PFI
  • Likelihood of material misstatement · extent of management-judgment impact
  • Sources & reliability of underlying data (budgets, debt agreements, industry publications, third-party stats)
  • Stability of entity's business · team experience with business, industry & PFI reporting
  • For hypothetical assumptions — verify implications taken into account (e.g. sales ↑ beyond capacity → additional plant / subcontracting); consistent with purpose; not clearly unrealistic

Presentation & disclosure checks

  • Presentation informative and not misleading
  • Accounting policies clearly disclosed in notes
  • Assumptions adequately disclosed — clear best-estimate vs hypothetical marker; for material + highly uncertain areas, disclose sensitivity
  • Date of preparation disclosed; management confirms assumptions appropriate as of that date
  • Range basis indicated, not biased / misleading
  • Any change in accounting policy vs recent historical FS — reason + effect disclosed

Exceptions / Conditions

Evidence on PFI is itself future-oriented and speculative — therefore an opinion on whether results will be achieved cannot be expressed.
Decline / withdraw when assumptions are clearly unrealistic or the PFI is inappropriate for its intended use.
Clause 3, Part I, Second Schedule, CA Act 1949 — CA in practice guilty of misconduct if he permits name to be used with an estimate of earnings contingent upon future transactions in a manner which may lead to belief that he vouches for accuracy. Clause does NOT preclude association with PFI provided the report discloses sources, basis & major assumptions and does not vouch for accuracy.

Report contents (a)–(m)

  • (a) Title · (b) Addressee · (c) Identification of PFI · (d) Reference to applicable SAs
  • (e) Statement that management is responsible for PFI incl. underlying assumptions
  • (f) Purpose / restricted distribution reference (when applicable)
  • (g) Examination was on a test basis of evidence supporting assumptions, amounts, disclosures
  • (h) Negative assurance — assumptions provide a reasonable basis
  • (i) Positive opinion — PFI properly prepared on basis of assumptions & presented per FRF
  • (j) Caveats on achievability · (k) Date · (l) Place · (m) Signature

Modification matrix

SituationReport action
Presentation / disclosure inadequate (e.g. highly-sensitive assumption not disclosed)Qualified / adverse or withdraw
One or more significant assumptions don't provide reasonable basisAdverse with reasons, or withdraw
Conditions preclude a necessary procedureDisclaimer describing scope limit, or withdraw

Conclusion / Reporting

Express negative assurance on reasonableness of assumptions + positive opinion on proper preparation & presentation; never vouch for accuracy of the forecast itself.

SAE 3402 Assurance Reports on Controls at a Service Organisation

Scope: Assurance engagements by a professional accountant in public practice to report, for use by user entities and their auditors, on controls at a service organisation relevant to user entities' internal control over financial reporting. Complements SA 402 — reports issued under this SAE are capable of providing appropriate evidence under SA 402.

Objective — verbatim

"The objectives of the service auditor are: (a) To obtain reasonable assurance about whether, in all material respects, based on suitable criteria: (i) The service organization's description of its system fairly presents the system as designed and implemented throughout the specified period (or in the case of a type 1 report, as at a specified date). (ii) The controls related to the control objectives stated in the service organization's description of its system were suitably designed throughout the specified period (or in the case of a type 1 report, as at a specified date). (iii) Where included in the scope of the engagement, the controls operated effectively to provide reasonable assurance that the control objectives stated in the service organization's description of its system were achieved throughout the specified period. (b) To report on the matters in (a) above in accordance with the service auditor's findings."

Key Definitions

  • Service organisation — third-party (or segment) providing services to user entities that are likely to be relevant to user entities' internal control as it relates to FR
  • User entity — entity that uses a service organisation; User auditor audits user entity's FS
  • Service auditor — professional accountant in public practice giving assurance report on controls at the service organisation (at the SO's request)
  • Controls at the SO — include aspects of user entities' info systems maintained by SO + aspects of SO's control environment / monitoring / control activities relevant to the services
  • Complementary user entity controls (CUECs) — controls the SO assumes the user will implement

Scope exclusions

SAE 3402 does not apply to engagements: (a) to report only on whether controls operated as described (without design / effectiveness opinion); or (b) to report on controls not related to services that are likely relevant to user entities' FR internal control (e.g. production, quality control).

Type 1 vs Type 2 — the headline comparison

ElementType 1 reportType 2 report
Reference pointAs at a specified dateThroughout a specified period

Design of controlsSuitably designed as at dateSuitably designed throughout period

Picks when …User only needs to understand design (point-in-time, new SO)User auditor plans to rely on controls for evidence

Engagement procedure — 12 steps

  • (1) Ethics & independence per assurance framework
  • (2) Determine appropriate persons within SO's management / governance to interact with
  • (3) Acceptance — capabilities & competence; criteria suitable & available to users; scope not so limited as to be useless; scope changes need reasonable justification
  • (4) Assess suitability of criteria for description, design, (Type 2) effectiveness
  • (5) Determine materiality for description, design, (Type 2) effectiveness
  • (6) Obtain understanding of SO's system incl. in-scope controls
  • (7) Obtain & read SO's description; evaluate fair presentation
  • (8) Identify controls necessary to achieve control objectives; assess design
  • (9) For Type 2 — test operating effectiveness throughout period
  • (10) Understand SO's internal audit function if any; use of IA work
  • (11) Written representations — reaffirm assertion; all info & access provided; disclose NOCLAR / fraud / uncorrected deviations, design deficiencies, instances where controls did not operate as described, subsequent events
  • (12) Subsequent events up to report date

Reporting — basic elements & modifications

  • Title (independent service auditor's assurance report) · addressee · identification of system description, assertion, un-covered parts, CUECs, subservice organisation (if any)
  • Criteria & party specifying control objectives
  • Restricted-use statement — report is for user entities & their auditors only
  • SO responsibility statement · service auditor responsibility · reference to SAE 3402
  • Summary of procedures & SAAE basis; for Type 1 — explicit statement that no opinion on operating effectiveness
  • Statement of limitations of controls; for Type 2 — risk of projecting to future periods
  • Positive opinion on fair presentation, suitable design, (Type 2) effectiveness
  • Type 2 — separate section / attachment: tests performed + results + deviations (report deviations even if control objective was achieved)
Modified opinion when: (a) description does not fairly present the system; (b) controls were not suitably designed; (c) Type 2 — controls tested did not operate effectively; (d) unable to obtain SAAE. Report must contain clear description of all reasons for modification.

Documentation

  • Sufficient for an experienced service auditor with no prior connection to understand N/T/E, results, SAAE, significant matters, judgments & conclusions

Conclusion / Reporting

Positive-form opinion, restricted-use; Type 2 adds tests + results; user auditor then consumes it under SA 402 to obtain SAAE on user-entity IC.

SAE 3410 Assurance Engagements on Greenhouse Gas Statements

Scope: Assurance engagements on an entity's GHG statement (emissions inventory).

SAE 3410 is not reproduced in the Ch 11 Final SM PDF (the PDF covers only SAE 3400, 3402 and 3420). ICAI-verbatim Objective and detailed requirements are therefore not quoted here — no fabrication. Refer to the bare standard if tested in a case study.

Unique GHG issues to remember (keywords only)

  • Scope 1 / 2 / 3 emissions — direct, purchased energy, value-chain
  • Estimation uncertainty — emission factors, activity data quality
  • Practitioner may be reasonable or limited assurance engagement
  • Materiality expressed in tCO₂-e, not currency
  • Specialist / expert use likely (engineering, environmental science)

SAE 3420 Assurance Engagements to Report on the Compilation of Pro-Forma Financial Information Included in a Prospectus

Scope: Reasonable assurance engagements by a practitioner to report on the responsible party's compilation of pro-forma FI included in a prospectus. Applies where such reporting is required by securities law / stock-exchange regulation, or is generally accepted practice in the jurisdiction.

Objective — verbatim

"The objectives of the practitioner in accordance with SAE 3420 are: (a) To obtain reasonable assurance about whether the pro forma financial information has been compiled, in all material respects, by the responsible party on the basis of the applicable criteria and (b) To report in accordance with the practitioner's findings."

Key Definitions

  • Pro-forma financial information — FI shown with adjustments to illustrate impact of an event / transaction on unadjusted FI as if the event had occurred / transaction had been undertaken at an earlier date selected for the illustration
  • Applicable criteria — criteria used by the responsible party in compiling pro-forma FI; may be set by recognised standard-setter / law / regulation, or developed by responsible party where none exist
  • Pro-forma adjustments — (a) adjustments illustrating impact as-if event occurred at earlier date; and (b) adjustments necessary for pro-forma FI to be compiled on a basis consistent with the reporting entity's FRF & accounting policies
  • Responsible party — the entity / its management compiling the pro-forma FI (not the practitioner)

Nature & purpose

  • Pro-forma FI is used in offer documents to demonstrate effect of a significant transaction on the Issuer's assets, liabilities, earnings
  • Does NOT represent actual financial position / performance / cash flows
  • Practitioner has no responsibility to compile — sole responsibility is to report on whether compilation was done per applicable criteria

Engagement steps — 5-stage flow

  • (1) Acceptance — practitioner competence; applicable criteria suitable & not likely misleading; prescribed opinion wording achievable; treatment of modified audit / review opinion or EOM on source FI permitted; understanding of entity & (if acquiree FI never audited/reviewed) of acquiree; responsible-party acknowledgement of its responsibilities (disclose criteria, compile per criteria, provide access to records, additional info, entity personnel & advisors, acquiree individuals)
  • (2) Planning & performing — assess suitability of criteria (Framework for Assurance Engagements); materiality wrt compilation per criteria; understand how responsible party compiled; evidence on appropriateness of source of unadjusted FI; if no audit/review on source — perform procedures to be satisfied; verify responsible party extracted unadjusted FI appropriately; evidence on appropriateness of pro-forma adjustments; evaluate presentation; read other information in the prospectus for material inconsistency
  • (3) Written representations — responsible party has (a) identified all appropriate pro-forma adjustments necessary to illustrate the event/transaction impact at the illustration date; (b) compiled pro-forma FI in all material respects on the basis of applicable criteria
  • (4) Opinion — unmodified when compiled, in all material respects, per criteria; modified per Framework; if law precludes publication with modified opinion and responsible party won't make changes → withdraw or seek legal advice; Emphasis of Matter permitted if matter fundamental to users' understanding & doesn't affect compilation
  • (5) Assurance report — elements below; documentation as in all assurance engagements

Report — basic elements

  • (a) Title (independent assurance report) · (b) Addressee(s)
  • (c) Introductory paragraphs — identify the pro-forma FI; source of unadjusted FI & whether audit/review report thereon published; period / date of pro-forma FI; reference to applicable criteria & source of criteria
  • (d) Responsible party's responsibility — compile per applicable criteria
  • (e) Practitioner's responsibility — express opinion on compilation; not updating any report on historical FI; no audit/review of that historical FI; purpose is solely to illustrate — no assurance that actual outcome would have been as presented
  • (f) Engagement performed per SAE 3420; ethics + reasonable assurance procedures
  • (g) Reasonable-assurance procedures assess whether criteria provide a reasonable basis for the significant effects directly attributable to the event/transaction, and whether (i) related adjustments give appropriate effect to those criteria and (ii) pro-forma FI reflects proper application of those adjustments to the unadjusted FI; procedures depend on judgment; also evaluates overall presentation
  • (h) Opinion — "pro-forma FI has been compiled, in all material respects, on the basis of the applicable criteria" OR "properly compiled on the basis stated" (equivalent)
  • (i) Signature · (j) Date · (k) Place

Exceptions / Conditions

Practitioner does NOT provide assurance that the actual outcome of the event/transaction would have been as presented — pro-forma FI does not represent actual financial position, performance or cash flows.
If source FI report carries a modified opinion / review conclusion / EOM, evaluate whether relevant law permits its use or reference in the practitioner's report on the pro-forma FI.

Conclusion / Reporting

Positive-form reasonable-assurance opinion on compilation per criteria; EOM permitted; modification → withdraw or legal advice where law precludes publication of a modified report.

CA Act guardrail — SAE 3400 & 3420 reports

A CA associating with any prospective / pro-forma FI must not vouch for accuracy of the forecast / projection / pro-forma outcome. The report must disclose sources, basis, major assumptions. Breach attracts professional misconduct under Clause 3, Part I, Second Schedule, CA Act 1949.

5 Exam Triggers — spot these phrasings, invoke these SAEs

  1. "Company is in a start-up phase preparing a 7-year feasibility for a term-loan banker, with hypothetical assumptions about a product yet to be launched" → SAE 3400 — this is a projection (hypothetical / mix), not a forecast; ensure hypothetical assumptions are consistent with intended use and not clearly unrealistic; negative assurance on assumptions + positive on preparation.
  2. "Depreciation in the projection was computed per Income-tax Act whereas historical FS follow Companies Act; no disclosure of the change" → SAE 3400 — PFI not on a consistent basis with historical FS; presentation / disclosure inadequate; issue a qualified / adverse opinion or withdraw (Test Your Understanding 1).
  3. "User-entity auditor wants assurance on design and operating effectiveness throughout the year of an outsourced payroll-processing service organisation's controls" → SAE 3402 Type 2 report; user auditor relies under SA 402; Type 1 (design-only, point-in-time) would be insufficient.
  4. "Service organisation's description assumes user entity itself implements certain controls (batch reconciliations)" → SAE 3402 — complementary user entity controls (CUECs); service auditor's report must state CUECs were not evaluated; control objectives achievable only if CUECs are suitably designed & operating.
  5. "Issuer's prospectus shows pro-forma FI for a recent acquisition; historical FS source carries an EOM; management wants the practitioner's report to certify the actual post-merger performance" → SAE 3420 — practitioner reports only on compilation per applicable criteria; no assurance on actual outcome; evaluate whether law permits reference to EOM on source FI; Clause 3, Pt I, Sch II, CA Act prohibits vouching for accuracy.
