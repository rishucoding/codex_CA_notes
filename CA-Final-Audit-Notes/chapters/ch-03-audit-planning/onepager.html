SAs at a glance

SACore trigger / exception
300Strategy (scope/timing/direction) → Plan (N/T/E) → Document. Auditor-owned; avoid predictability.
600Principal uses other auditor. Consider competence if non-ICAI. State division of responsibility. Not for joint/predecessor.

Strategy vs Plan

StrategyScope · Timing · Direction; first; characteristics · reporting obj · resources.
PlanNature · Timing · Extent; RAP (SA 315) · further procedures (SA 330) · other SA-required.

Must-remember keywords

  • Preliminary (3): continuance (SA 220) · ethics · terms (SA 210)
  • Change triggers: unexpected events · changed conditions · contradicting evidence
  • SA 600 acceptance: materiality · knowledge · RoMM · additional procedures
  • SA 610 three-test: organisational status (objectivity) · competence · systematic & disciplined approach
  • SA 620 written-agreement: nature/scope/objectives · roles · communications · confidentiality

5 Exam Triggers

  1. Never visited client; generic checklist; sample size deferred → SA 300 plan & document fail.
  2. Mid-audit IC weakness → lower materiality → strategy first, then plan (SA 320/450).
  3. Component auditor issues modified report; principal disagrees → SA 600 — consider modifying own report; state division of responsibility; qualified/disclaimer if SAAE unobtainable.
  4. High-judgment + high-RoMM inventory obsolescence; IA silent → SA 610 — less reliance, more direct procedures; communicate to TCWG.
  5. Actuary is CFO's cousin; no written terms; referenced in unmodified report → SA 620 — objectivity threat; mandatory written agreement; no reference unless law requires (with caveat).

Reporting bottom-lines

  • SA 300: auditor-owned, documented, changes + reasons.
  • SA 600: division of responsibility stated; qualified/disclaimer on scope limitation.
  • SA 610/620: sole responsibility undiminished; modify opinion (SA 705) if IA/expert work inadequate and gap unbridgeable.
