Chapter 2 — General Auditing Principles & Auditor's Responsibilities

ICAI SM Final P3 May 2026 · ~12–15 marks Covers SA 200 (umbrella) · SA 210 · SA 230 · SA 240 · SA 250 · SA 260 · SA 265 · SA 299 · SA 402

Divergence from SPEC §11 map. The ICAI SM PDF for Ch 2 gives full treatment only to SA 240, 250, 260, 299 and SA 402. The PDF expressly notes that SA 200, SA 210, SA 230 content is covered in depth at the Intermediate level and at Final only application/case-study is expected. SA 265 is not covered in this chapter's PDF. The cards below therefore give brief revision blocks for SA 200/210/230/265 (no ICAI-verbatim Objective available in this PDF for those four) and full treatment to SA 240/250/260/299. SA 402 has also been included because the PDF devotes an entire section to it.

Chapter map

This chapter sets the operating principles of every audit. SA 200 is the umbrella — reasonable assurance, professional skepticism, inherent limitations. SA 210 locks the engagement before fieldwork; SA 230 governs the file during. SA 240 and SA 250 are the two "watch-out" SAs — fraud and non-compliance with laws.

SA 260 (communication with TCWG) and SA 265 (deficiencies) are the outbound-communication pair. SA 299 is the special-case SA for joint auditors. SA 402 (service organisations) appears in the PDF and is appended for completeness.

SA cluster overview

SA 200 Overall Objectives of the Independent Auditor

Scope: Umbrella SA for every audit of financial statements conducted under SAs. PDF status: per the note at the end of the chapter, SA 200 is covered in depth at Intermediate level; at Final, only application in case studies is expected.

Objective

ICAI-verbatim Objective sentence is not reproduced in the Ch 2 Final SM PDF. The PDF only states (line 97): "SA 200 establishes the independent auditor's overall responsibilities when conducting an audit of financial statements in accordance with SAs." — not fabricating a verbatim Objective here.

Key Definitions (revision)

  • Reasonable assurance — high, not absolute, level of assurance
  • Material misstatement — could influence economic decisions of users
  • Professional skepticism — questioning mind, alert to contrary evidence
  • Professional judgment — training + experience + ethical requirements applied

Key Requirements

  • Ethical requirements · including independence, complied throughout
  • Professional skepticism · planning and performance
  • Sufficient appropriate audit evidence · to reduce audit risk to acceptably low
  • Conduct in accordance with SAs · comply with every relevant SA in its entirety
  • Comply with relevant requirements unless SA not relevant / no conditions addressed

Exceptions / Conditions

Inherent limitations: nature of financial reporting, nature of audit procedures, need for audit to be conducted within reasonable time & cost — absolute assurance is not attainable.

Auditor's Responsibilities

  • Obtain reasonable assurance that FS are free from material misstatement (fraud or error)
  • Report on the FS & communicate as required by SAs
  • Comply with Code of Ethics & relevant SAs

Conclusion / Reporting

Express an opinion on whether the FS are prepared, in all material respects, in accordance with the applicable FRF.

SA 210 Agreeing the Terms of Audit Engagements

Scope: Auditor's responsibilities in agreeing terms with management / TCWG before accepting/continuing an engagement. PDF status: deferred to Intermediate; Final tests application.

Objective

ICAI-verbatim Objective for SA 210 is not quoted in the Ch 2 Final SM PDF (deferred to Intermediate). Not fabricated here.

Key Definitions

  • Preconditions for an audit — acceptable FRF + management acknowledgment of responsibilities
  • Engagement letter — written agreement on terms

Key Requirements

  • Confirm preconditions exist · acceptable FRF · premise of responsibilities
  • Record agreed terms in an engagement letter or other written form
  • For recurring audits · assess if revised letter needed (change in senior management, nature/size, law/FRF)
  • Do not agree to a change in terms where no reasonable justification
  • If preconditions absent · decline the engagement

Exceptions / Conditions

If law/regulation prescribes terms in sufficient detail, engagement letter may be recorded by way of a written statement that the law/regulation applies & management acknowledges its responsibilities.

Auditor's Responsibilities

  • Confirm preconditions; document terms; refuse unjustified scope reductions mid-engagement

Conclusion / Reporting

If terms cannot be agreed, do not accept the engagement (unless required by law).

SA 230 Audit Documentation

Scope: Auditor's responsibility to prepare the audit file. PDF status: deferred to Intermediate; Final tests application.

Objective

ICAI-verbatim Objective for SA 230 is not quoted in the Ch 2 Final SM PDF (deferred to Intermediate). Not fabricated here.

Key Definitions

  • Audit documentation — record of procedures, evidence, conclusions (working papers)
  • Audit file — folders/storage containing records for a specific engagement
  • Experienced auditor — has practical audit experience + understanding of audit processes, SAs, regulatory environment, industry

Key Requirements

  • Documentation sufficient for an experienced auditor (no prior connection) to understand N/T/E, results, conclusions, significant matters
  • Record identifying characteristics of items tested · who performed · who reviewed · dates
  • Assembly of the final file · timely basis after report date (SQC 1: ≤ 60 days)
  • After assembly, no deletion/discarding before retention period ends (SQC 1: ≥ 7 yrs)
  • Document departure from a relevant requirement — reason + alternative procedures performed

Exceptions / Conditions

Post-assembly modifications must record: reason, when & by whom made, reviewed. Never alter / discard to conceal.

Auditor's Responsibilities

  • Timely prep, retention, confidentiality & integrity of the audit file

Conclusion / Reporting

Documentation evidences that the audit was planned and performed in accordance with SAs.

SA 240 The Auditor's Responsibilities Relating to Fraud in an Audit of Financial Statements

Scope: Auditor's responsibilities relating to fraud in an FS audit — expands SA 315 & SA 330 in relation to risks of material misstatement due to fraud.

Objective

"(a) To identify and assess the risks of material misstatement in the financial statements due to fraud; (b) To obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence about the assessed risks of material misstatement due to fraud, through designing and implementing appropriate responses; and (c) To respond appropriately to identified or suspected fraud."

Key Definitions

  • Fraud — intentional act by management/TCWG/employees/third parties involving deception to obtain unjust or illegal advantage
  • Fraud risk factors — events/conditions indicating incentive/pressure, opportunity, or rationalization
  • Two species: fraudulent financial reporting & misappropriation of assets
  • Fraud triangle: incentive/pressure · opportunity · rationalization

Key Requirements

  • Maintain professional skepticism throughout the audit, notwithstanding past honest dealings
  • Engagement team discussion — where FS may be susceptible to fraud, how fraud might occur
  • Risk assessment procedures to identify risks of material misstatement due to fraud; presume risk in revenue recognition (rebut with documented reasons)
  • Mandatory response to management override of controls: test journal entries, review estimates for bias, scrutinise significant unusual transactions
  • Obtain written representations from management & TCWG on fraud prevention/detection and disclosure of known/suspected fraud
  • Timely communication — management (operational), TCWG (management fraud / material fraud), regulators where duty arises

Exceptions / Conditions

Risk of not detecting fraud > risk of not detecting error. Management fraud risk > employee fraud risk (override + collusion + seniority). Confidentiality duty may be overridden by law (e.g. banks must report to RBI; s.143(12) requires fraud > ₹1 crore reported to Central Government within prescribed time).

Auditor's Responsibilities vs Management / TCWG

  • Management + TCWG: primary responsibility to prevent & detect fraud — culture of honesty, oversight
  • Auditor: reasonable assurance that FS are free of material misstatement (fraud or error); does not make legal determinations of fraud
  • If exceptional circumstances question ability to continue — determine legal/professional duties, consider withdrawal (where permitted), report

Conclusion / Reporting

If FS materially misstated due to fraud → evaluate implications for opinion (SA 705); communicate to TCWG & regulators; document risk decisions & rebuttal of revenue-recognition fraud presumption.

SA 250 Consideration of Laws and Regulations in an Audit of Financial Statements

Scope: Auditor's responsibility to consider laws & regulations when performing an FS audit. Does not apply to assurance engagements engaged specifically to test compliance.

Objective

"(a) To obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence regarding compliance with the provisions of those laws and regulations generally recognised to have a direct effect on the determination of material amounts and disclosures in the financial statements; (b) To perform specified audit procedures to help identify instances of non-compliance with other laws and regulations that may have a material effect on the financial statements; and (c) To respond appropriately to non-compliance or suspected non-compliance with laws and regulations identified during the audit."

Key Definitions

  • Non-compliance — acts of omission/commission by the entity, intentional or unintentional, contrary to prevailing laws/regulations
  • Category (a) laws — direct effect on FS amounts/disclosures (e.g. tax, labour)
  • Category (b) laws — no direct effect but fundamental to operations/going concern (licences, solvency, environment)

Key Requirements

  • Obtain general understanding of the legal & regulatory framework & how entity complies
  • For Category (a): obtain SAAE regarding compliance
  • For Category (b): inquire of management/TCWG + inspect correspondence with regulators
  • Remain alert that other procedures may surface non-compliance
  • Obtain written representations on disclosure of known/suspected non-compliance
  • Where suspected NOCLAR: discuss with management/TCWG; legal advice if needed; evaluate effect on opinion

Exceptions / Conditions

Auditor is not responsible for preventing NOCLAR and cannot be expected to detect all NOCLAR. Whether an act constitutes non-compliance is ultimately a matter for a court of law.
Confidentiality duty may be overridden where statute requires reporting to regulators (e.g. banks → RBI).

Auditor's Responsibilities (three-tier reporting)

  • To TCWG: communicate matters (other than clearly inconsequential); escalate to next-higher authority if TCWG involved
  • In the auditor's report: material & not reflected → qualified / adverse per SA 705; precluded from SAAE → qualified / disclaimer
  • To regulators / enforcement authorities: determine if reporting duty outside the entity exists

Conclusion / Reporting

Document identified/suspected NOCLAR, discussions, and conclusions. Modify opinion per SA 705 when material effect not reflected or when SAAE unobtainable.

SA 260 Communication with Those Charged with Governance

Scope: Auditor's responsibility to communicate with TCWG in an FS audit. Two-way communication to promote oversight & audit quality.

Objective

"(a) To communicate clearly with those charged with governance responsibilities of the auditor in relation to the financial statement audit, and an overview of the planned scope and timing of the audit; (b) To obtain from those charged with governance information relevant to the audit; (c) To provide those charged with governance with timely observations arising from the audit that are significant and relevant to their responsibility to oversee the financial reporting process; and (d) To promote effective two-way communication between the auditor and those charged with governance."

Key Definitions

  • Those charged with governance (TCWG) — person(s)/organisation(s) responsible for overseeing strategic direction & accountability, including oversight of financial reporting process
  • Management — executive responsibility for conduct of operations

Key Requirements — matters to communicate

  • The auditor's responsibilities in relation to the FS audit (non-relieving of management's)
  • Planned scope & timing, including significant risks identified
  • Significant findings — qualitative aspects of accounting practices, significant difficulties, disagreements with management, written representations sought, circumstances affecting report form/content
  • Auditor independence (listed entities) — statement + relationships & fees + safeguards
  • Form, timing, content of communication agreed; written where oral inadequate
  • Evaluate adequacy of two-way communication; if not adequate, consider impact on audit & act

Exceptions / Conditions

If all TCWG are involved in management, certain items (e.g. significant matters discussed with management, written reps requested) need not be re-communicated.
Care to not compromise audit effectiveness by pre-disclosing N/T/E of detailed procedures where TCWG manage the entity.

Auditor's Responsibilities

  • Identify appropriate persons within governance structure
  • Communicate on a timely basis; document orally-made communications & retain written ones

Conclusion / Reporting

Inadequate two-way communication → may modify risk assessment, obtain additional evidence, or escalate; last resort → consider withdrawal where permitted.

SA 265 Communicating Deficiencies in Internal Control to Those Charged with Governance and Management

Scope: Outbound communication of control deficiencies identified during the audit. PDF status: SA 265 is not covered in the Ch 2 Final SM PDF (flagged up-top).

Objective

ICAI-verbatim Objective for SA 265 is not reproduced in the Ch 2 Final SM PDF. Not fabricated here. Treat as refresher from SA 315/Intermediate-level materials.

Key Definitions

  • Deficiency in internal control — control missing or not designed/implemented to prevent/detect misstatements on timely basis
  • Significant deficiency — of sufficient importance to merit TCWG attention (professional judgment)

Key Requirements

  • Determine whether identified deficiencies are significant deficiencies
  • Communicate significant deficiencies in writing to TCWG on timely basis
  • Communicate to management (appropriate level) in writing — other deficiencies meriting attention
  • Communication to include description, potential effects, sufficient info for context, explanation that communication is for audit purposes only

Exceptions / Conditions

Communication not appropriate to report directly to management if it would be inappropriate (e.g. calls into question management's integrity/competence) — then report directly to TCWG.

Auditor's Responsibilities

  • Identify → classify → communicate (written); re-communicate if prior-period deficiencies not remediated

Conclusion / Reporting

Written communication to TCWG/management; does not modify the auditor's opinion unless combined effect triggers SA 705.

SA 299 Joint Audit of Financial Statements

Scope: Principles for effective conduct of a joint audit to achieve SA 200 overall objectives. Does not cover principal–component auditor relationships (that is SA 600).

Objective

"(a) To lay down broad principles for the joint auditors in conducting the joint audit. (b) To provide a uniform approach to the process of joint audit. (c) To identify the distinct areas of work and coverage thereof by each joint auditor. (d) To identify individual responsibility and joint responsibility of the joint auditors in relation to audit."

Key Definitions

  • Joint audit — audit of FS of an entity by two or more auditors appointed with the objective of issuing the audit report
  • Joint auditors — such auditors

Key Requirements

  • Engagement partner & key members of each JA jointly plan — establish overall audit strategy
  • Develop a joint audit plan: identify division of areas, common areas, reporting objectives, significant factors, resources
  • Work-allocation document signed by all JAs & communicated to TCWG
  • Obtain common engagement letter & common management representation letter
  • Communicate material matters between JAs in writing before completion
  • Issue a common audit report; disagreement → separate report, cross-refer under Other Matter Paragraph (SA 706)

Exceptions / Conditions — responsibility split

Separate & specific responsibility: for the work allocated to that JA — including obtaining info from management for that division/zone/unit.
Joint & several responsibility: (a) undivided work done by all JAs; (b) decisions taken by all JAs on common areas (N/T/E); (c) matters brought to notice of any JA with agreement among JAs; (d) FS compliance with statutes; (e) presentation & disclosure per FRF; (f) audit report compliance with statutes, SAs & ICAI pronouncements.
A JA is entitled to assume other JAs did their part per SAs & would flag departures — no duty to review other JAs' work.

Auditor's Responsibilities

  • Each JA — individual study of IC & risk for their allocated area; determine N/T/E of procedures for that area
  • All JAs — communicate jointly with TCWG on modifications / EOM / OM expected in report

Conclusion / Reporting

Common report where JAs agree; separate reports cross-referenced under "Other Matter" where they disagree. No joint auditor is bound by the majority view.

SA 402 Audit Considerations Relating to an Entity Using a Service Organisation

Scope: User auditor's responsibility for SAAE when a user entity uses services of one or more service organisations. Expands how SA 315 & SA 330 apply. (Included because the PDF devotes §6 to it; strictly Ch 4 territory under SPEC §11.)

Objective

"(a) To obtain an understanding of the nature and significance of the services provided by the service organisation and their effect on the user entity's internal control relevant to the audit, sufficient to identify and assess the risks of material misstatement; and (b) To design and perform audit procedures responsive to those risks."

Key Definitions

  • Service organisation — third party providing services that are part of user entity's info system relevant to FR
  • User entity / user auditor vs service organisation / service auditor
  • Type 1 report — design & implementation at a date
  • Type 2 report — design + operating effectiveness over a period
  • Complementary user entity controls — controls the SO assumes user will implement

Key Requirements

  • Obtain understanding of nature & significance of services and their effect on user's IC
  • If insufficient: use Type 1 / Type 2 report, contact SO, visit SO, or use another auditor
  • Assess service auditor competence & independence; adequacy of standards under which report issued
  • Identify & evaluate complementary user entity controls
  • Respond to assessed risks (SA 330): obtain SAAE from user records or perform further procedures / use another auditor

Exceptions / Conditions

Service auditor's professional competence need not be separately evaluated if the service auditor is a member of ICAI.

Auditor's Responsibilities — reporting

  • Unable to obtain SAAE → modify opinion per SA 705
  • Do not refer to work of the service auditor in an unmodified opinion
  • If reference required by law/regulation to explain a modification → indicate it does not diminish user auditor's responsibility

Conclusion / Reporting

Evidence obtained via Type 1/Type 2 + direct work → form opinion on user entity's FS; reference to service auditor only if legally required & with proper caveats.

5 Exam Triggers — spot these phrasings, invoke these SAs

  1. "Management refused to allow the auditor to communicate the audit plan & significant risks with the Audit Committee/Board; the MD insisted all matters be routed only through him" → SA 260 — auditor must identify appropriate TCWG persons, evaluate adequacy of two-way communication, and consider impact on audit & on opinion (scope limitation).
  2. "Auditor detected an off-book sale of ₹ 43 crore with proceeds partly deposited in the CFO's personal account; CFO argues the sum is immaterial" → SA 240 — management fraud / override of controls; rebutted revenue-recognition fraud risk; s.143(12) mandatory reporting to Central Government for fraud > ₹ 1 crore.
  3. "DGGI issues show-cause for fictitious invoicing; IRDAI also notified; management withholds documents claiming sub-judice" → SA 250 — suspected NOCLAR with possible material effect; obtain legal advice, evaluate SAAE, consider qualified/adverse or disclaimer under SA 705, communicate to TCWG & consider duty to report to regulator.
  4. "Four firms appointed as joint auditors of a PSU bank; work divided zone-wise; IT general controls kept as common area; DES & Associates' team fails to test batch-processing controls" → SA 299 — common/undivided areas → joint & several liability of all joint auditors; allocated-zone lapses remain individual responsibility of the firm concerned.
  5. "Joint auditors disagree on whether to modify opinion in respect of related-party disclosures; three want unmodified, one wants qualified" → SA 299 — dissenting JA issues a separate audit report; cross-refer via Other Matter Paragraph (SA 706); majority view does not bind the dissenting joint auditor.
