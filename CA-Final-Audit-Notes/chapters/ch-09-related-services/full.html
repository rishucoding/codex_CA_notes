Chapter 9 — Related Services (SRS 4400, SRS 4410)

ICAI SM Final P3 May 2026 · ~6–8 marks Covers SRS 4400 (Agreed-Upon Procedures) · SRS 4410 (Compilation Engagements)

Chapter map

Related Services are the non-assurance siblings of audit/review. CAs are often asked to do work that does not involve an opinion on truth & fairness. The AASB has issued two SRSs for such work: SRS 4400 (Agreed-Upon Procedures on financial information — "AUP") and SRS 4410 (Compilation Engagements).

Both are non-assurance engagements: no opinion, no conclusion, no verification of accuracy/completeness. The practitioner either reports factual findings (4400) or assists in preparation & presentation of financial information and issues a compilation report (4410). Independence is not a precondition (disclose if not independent under 4400).

SRS 4400 Engagements to Perform Agreed-Upon Procedures Regarding Financial Information

Scope: Guidance on the auditor's professional responsibilities when engaged to perform agreed-upon procedures regarding financial information, and on the form and content of the report issued. Person performing related services need not be the entity's FS auditor.

Objective (ICAI verbatim)

"The objective of an agreed-upon procedures engagement is for the auditor to carry out procedures of an audit nature to which the auditor and the entity and any appropriate third parties have agreed and to report on factual findings."
"As the auditor simply provides a report of the factual findings of agreed upon procedures, no assurance is provided by him in his report. Instead, users of the report assess for themselves the procedures and the findings reported by the auditor and draw their own conclusions from the work done by the auditor."

Key Definitions

  • Agreed-upon procedures engagement — auditor issues a report of factual findings, based on specified procedures performed on specified subject matter of specified elements, accounts or items of an FS
  • Factual findings — results of the specific procedures; reported as such without assurance
  • Specified parties — entity + any appropriate third parties who agreed the procedures; recipients of the restricted report

General Principles

  • Comply with ICAI Code of Ethics: integrity · objectivity · professional competence & due care · confidentiality · professional conduct · technical standards
  • Independence not required — but if terms/objective mandate it, comply; if not independent, state so in the report of factual findings
  • Plan the work so effective engagement is performed
  • Carry out the agreed procedures and use evidence obtained as the basis for factual findings
  • Document matters supporting the findings & showing engagement was per SRS + terms

Defining the Terms — matters to agree

  • (a) Nature of engagement — procedures do not constitute an audit or review and no assurance will be expressed
  • (b) Stated purpose of the engagement
  • (c) Identification of the financial information to which AUP applied
  • (d) Nature, timing & extent (N/T/E) of specific procedures
  • (e) Limitations on distribution of the report; if such limitation conflicts with legal requirements → do not accept the engagement

Send an engagement letter listing the procedures, and a statement that distribution of the report will be restricted to specified parties.

Procedures & Evidence — typical techniques

  • Inquiry & analysis
  • Recomputation, comparison & other clerical-accuracy checks
  • Observation · inspection · obtaining confirmations

Reporting — required contents of the report of factual findings

  • (a) Title; (b) addressee (ordinarily the appointing authority);
  • (c) Identification of specific financial or non-financial info to which AUP applied;
  • (d) Statement that procedures performed were those agreed-upon with the recipient;
  • (e) Statement that engagement was performed per the SRS applicable to AUP;
  • (f) Identification of the purpose for which AUP were performed;
  • (g) Listing of the specific procedures performed;
  • (h) Description of factual findings, with sufficient details of errors & exceptions found;
  • (i) Statement that the procedures do not constitute an audit or review and, as such, no assurance is expressed;
  • (j) Statement that had additional procedures / an audit / a review been performed, other matters might have come to light that would have been reported;
  • (k) Statement that the report is restricted to parties that agreed to the procedures;
  • (l) Statement (when applicable) that report relates only to specified elements / accounts / items / information and does not extend to the FS taken as a whole;
  • (m) Date; (n) place of signature; (o) auditor's signature.

Exceptions / Conditions

Independence is not a requirement, but where the auditor is not independent and terms do not require it, a statement to that effect must be made in the report.
If distribution restriction would conflict with legal requirements → the auditor would not accept the engagement.
"Auditor" in SRS 4400 does not imply the person be the entity's FS auditor — any appropriately qualified practitioner may undertake AUP.

Conclusion / Reporting

Deliver a factual-findings report with the 15 elements above. Users read the procedures & findings and draw their own conclusions. The engagement is non-assurance; misuse (read as audit/review) is avoided by the restricted-distribution & "no assurance" statements.

SRS 4410 Compilation Engagements

Scope: Practitioner's responsibilities when engaged to assist management with preparation & presentation of historical financial information without obtaining any assurance on that information, and to report on the engagement per this SRS. Non-historical / non-financial compilations — apply this SRS with necessary adaptation.

Objectives (ICAI verbatim)

"The practitioner's objectives in a compilation engagement under this SRS are to: (a) Apply accounting and financial reporting expertise to assist management in the preparation and presentation of financial information in accordance with an applicable financial reporting framework based on information provided by management; and (b) Report in accordance with the requirements of this SRS."
"A compilation engagement is not an assurance engagement. A compilation engagement does not require the practitioner to verify the accuracy or completeness of the information provided by management for the compilation, or otherwise to gather evidence to express an audit opinion or a review conclusion on the preparation of the financial information."

Key Definitions

  • Compilation engagement — practitioner applies accounting & FR expertise to assist management in preparation & presentation of FI per an applicable FRF, and issues a report
  • Applicable FRF — may range from simple entity-specific basis to established FR standards; chosen per entity nature & intended use
  • Special purpose FRF — used where FI prepared for a specific purpose; report includes an explanatory paragraph

Scope — intended purposes

  • Mandatory periodic financial reporting under law/regulation, if any
  • Non-mandatory: (i) for mgmt / TCWG internal use; (ii) for external parties under contract (e.g. grant funding body); (iii) for transactional purposes — M&A, changes in ownership/financing structure

Ethical Requirements

  • Comply with relevant ethical requirements; identify & address threats per Code of Ethics
  • Independence not required (non-assurance), unless laws/regulations specify

Engagement Acceptance & Continuance

Do not accept unless terms are agreed with mgmt (and engaging party if different), including:

  • (a) Intended use & distribution restrictions;
  • (b) Identification of the applicable FRF;
  • (c) Objective & scope of the compilation;
  • (d) Practitioner's responsibilities — including ethical compliance;
  • (e) Mgmt's responsibilities — FI & its preparation; internal control; accuracy & completeness of records/information; judgments (even where practitioner assisted);
  • (f) Expected form and content of the report.

Record terms in an engagement letter or other written agreement before performing. For recurring engagements: reassess whether terms need revision and whether to remind mgmt of existing terms.

Performing the Engagement

  • Obtain sufficient understanding of (a) entity's business, operations, accounting system & records; (b) applicable FRF and its industry application
  • Compile FI using records, documents, explanations & significant judgments provided by management
  • Discuss significant judgments (where practitioner assisted) with mgmt / TCWG
  • Before completion, read the compiled FI in light of understanding of entity & FRF
  • If information is incomplete, inaccurate or unsatisfactory → bring to mgmt's attention; request additional / corrected information
  • If mgmt fails to provide records/info/judgments → withdraw and inform mgmt & TCWG of reasons
  • If during engagement practitioner becomes aware that: (a) FI does not adequately refer to / describe FRF; (b) amendments required for FI not to be materially misstated; (c) FI is otherwise misleading → propose appropriate amendments
  • If mgmt declines proposed amendments → withdraw; if withdrawal not possible → determine professional & legal responsibilities
  • Obtain mgmt's (or TCWG's) acknowledgement of responsibility for the final version of the compiled FI

The Practitioner's Report — mandatory contents

  • (a) Title; (b) addressee(s) per engagement terms;
  • (c) Statement that practitioner has compiled the FI based on information provided by management;
  • (d) Description of management's responsibilities (compilation & FI);
  • (e) Identification of applicable FRF; if special purpose FRF — reference to its description;
  • (f) Identification of the FI — titles of elements, date/period;
  • (g) Description of practitioner's responsibilities, including engagement per this SRS and compliance with ethical requirements;
  • (h) Description of what a compilation entails per this SRS;
  • (i) Explanations that — (i) since compilation is not an assurance engagement, practitioner is not required to verify accuracy/completeness; (ii) accordingly, practitioner does not express an audit opinion or a review conclusion;
  • (j) If special purpose FRF — explanatory paragraph: (i) purpose & intended users (or reference to a note); (ii) caution that FI may not be suitable for other purposes;
  • (k) Date; (l) practitioner's signature; (m) place of signature.

Engagement-Level Quality Control & Documentation

  • Engagement partner takes responsibility for overall quality and for performance per firm's QC policies/procedures
  • SQC 1 applies to firms in respect of compilation engagements (SRS 4410 is an Engagement Standard)
  • Document: (a) significant matters & how addressed; (b) record of how compiled FI reconciles with underlying records; (c) final compiled FI + acknowledged responsibility + the report

Exceptions / Conditions

Compilation is not an assurance engagement — practitioner does not verify accuracy/completeness and does not gather evidence to express an opinion/conclusion.
If mgmt refuses amendments for FI that is materially misstated or misleading → withdraw. If withdrawal impossible → determine applicable professional / legal responsibilities.
Management retains responsibility for the FI and the basis of preparation — including accounting policies, estimates, and application of judgment. Practitioner's assistance does not shift that responsibility.

Communication with Management & TCWG

  • Communicate timely all matters that, in the practitioner's professional judgment, merit attention of management or TCWG
  • Discuss significant judgments where practitioner assisted; obtain management's acknowledgement on the final version

Conclusion / Reporting

Issue a compilation report in writing with the 13 elements above. The report is not a vehicle to express an opinion or conclusion. Where FI cannot be corrected — withdraw; if withdrawal not possible, act on professional / legal duties and document the rationale in the engagement file.

Decision triage — which SRS fits?

5 Exam Triggers — spot these phrasings, invoke these SRSs

  1. "Management requests CA to evaluate validity of accounts receivables / trade receivables — prepare & send confirmations, analyse variations, submit a report per professional standards; CA is not the statutory auditor" → SRS 4400 AUP. Report must contain: procedures agreed with recipient, factual findings, "no assurance is expressed", "had additional procedures / an audit / a review been performed, other matters might have come to light", restricted-distribution statement, and "does not extend to the FS taken as a whole".
  2. "Client provides trial balance and asks for assistance in preparation & presentation of FS plus a report" → SRS 4410 Compilation. Not an assurance engagement; practitioner does not verify accuracy/completeness; report states "does not express an audit opinion or a review conclusion".
  3. "During compilation, CA finds inventory grossly understated; applicable standards not applied; partners say 'outside your scope — accept our certified figures'" → SRS 4410 §3.5. Propose amendments; if management declines → withdraw and inform mgmt/TCWG; if withdrawal impossible → determine professional & legal responsibilities.
  4. "AUP terms require that distribution of the report be unrestricted, conflicting with legal disclosure requirements" OR "the CA is not independent of the entity" → SRS 4400. If restriction would conflict with law → do not accept. If not independent → explicit statement of non-independence in the report.
  5. "During compilation, CA notices opening-balance errors / omission of material expenditure (e.g. new-unit costs) from compiled FI" → SRS 4410. First bring to management's attention & propose amendments (§3.5). Omission → FI otherwise misleading → integrity principle; if uncorrected → withdraw. SQC 1 applies; SA 220 does not.
