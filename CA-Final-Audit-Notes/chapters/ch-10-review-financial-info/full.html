Chapter 10 — Review of Financial Information

ICAI SM Final P3 May 2026 · ~6–8 marks Covers SRE 2400 · SRE 2410

Chapter map

A review is a limited assurance engagement — lower than audit. The practitioner performs inquiry and analytical procedures (no tests of controls, no substantive tests as default), gathers sufficient appropriate evidence for a limited conclusion, and reports in negative wording: "nothing has come to our attention". Expect around 6–8 marks combined on this chapter, where the MCQ-plus-descriptive pattern tests both the SRE 2400 vs SRE 2410 distinction and the exact negative-form conclusion language.

Two SREs: SRE 2400 — historical FS review by a practitioner who is not the entity's auditor; SRE 2410 — interim FI review by the entity's own independent auditor (think SEBI LODR Reg 33 quarterly review). SQC 1 applies.

SRE 2400 Engagements to Review Historical Financial Statements

Scope: Practitioner's responsibilities when engaged to review historical FS and the practitioner is not the auditor of the entity's FS. Also covers form and content of the review report. FS may be under general- or special-purpose framework.

Objective

"(a) Obtain limited assurance, primarily by making an inquiry and performing analytical procedures, about whether the financial statements as a whole are free from material misstatement, thereby enabling the practitioner to express a conclusion on whether anything has come to his attention that causes him to believe the financial statements are not prepared, in all material respects, in accordance with an applicable financial reporting framework; (b) Report on the financial statements as a whole and communicate, as required by this SRE."

Key Definitions

  • Review — a limited assurance engagement; engagement risk reduced to acceptable, but greater than reasonable assurance
  • Limited assurance — meaningful level; procedures at least sufficient to enhance intended users' confidence
  • Practitioner — professional accountant in practice performing the review; here, not the entity's auditor

Pre-acceptance — Preconditions

  • Acceptable FRF applied in preparation of FS (understand purpose & users for special-purpose FS)
  • Management acknowledges responsibility — for FS preparation & for internal control
  • Management to provide access to all relevant info, additional info on request, and unrestricted access to persons within the entity
  • Rational purpose exists; ethical requirements incl. independence will be met; information available & reliable; management integrity not in doubt; no crippling scope limitation

Key Requirements — performance

  • Materiality for FS as a whole — same concept as in audit, independent of assurance level; revise if new info emerges
  • Obtain understanding of entity & FRF to identify where material misstatements likely
  • Design & perform inquiry + analytical procedures — address all material items and focus on risk areas
  • Procedures for specific circumstances: related parties, fraud / NOCLAR, going concern, use of others' work, group FS
  • If matters suggest possible material misstatement → additional procedures (deeper inquiry / analytics, or substantive tests of detail / external confirmations)
  • Obtain written representations from management — responsibilities fulfilled, all transactions recorded, related parties, fraud, NOCLAR, going concern, subsequent events, commitments, non-monetary transactions

Exceptions / Conditions

Do not accept if: no rational purpose (e.g. law requires audit, not review); ethical/independence requirements will not be met; information unavailable/unreliable; doubts on management integrity; management imposes scope limitation likely to result in disclaimer.
If preconditions not met but engagement proceeds under law/regulation → do not reference "conducted in accordance with SRE 2400" in the report.
If management does not provide the mandatory written representations (responsibility for FS & that all transactions recorded) or integrity doubt arises → disclaim conclusion or withdraw.

Subsequent events

  • Events between FS date & report date requiring adjustment/disclosure → request management to correct
  • After report date, before FS issued: if fact would have changed report → discuss, determine whether amendment needed, inquire management's course
  • If management refuses after report issued → notify management & TCWG not to issue; if issued anyway, take steps to prevent reliance

Conclusion / Reporting — negative wording (ICAI verbatim)

"Based on our review, nothing has come to our attention that causes us to believe that the financial statements do not give a true and fair view (or do not present fairly, in all material respects), in accordance with the applicable financial reporting framework" (fair presentation framework); or "…that the financial statements are not prepared, in all material respects, in accordance with the applicable financial reporting framework" (compliance framework).

Modifications: use heading "Qualified Conclusion", "Adverse Conclusion" or "Disclaimer of Conclusion"; precede with a "Basis for …" paragraph that describes & quantifies the effect (or states impracticability). Material + not pervasive → qualified; material + pervasive → adverse (misstatement) or disclaimer (cannot obtain evidence).

Report — mandatory elements (short list)

  • Title identifying "independent practitioner" + review engagement; addressee; intro para identifying FS & period
  • Management's responsibility; practitioner's responsibility referencing SRE 2400
  • Description of review & its limitations — "limited assurance engagement", "inquiries + analytical", "substantially less than an audit under SAs", "no audit opinion expressed"
  • "Conclusion" heading with conclusion + reference to FRF; EOM / OM paragraphs as appropriate; date (not earlier than SAAE); signature; place

Documentation

Sufficient for an experienced practitioner with no prior connection to understand N/T/E of procedures, results & conclusions, significant matters & judgments, who-did-what-when, and how any inconsistent information was addressed.

SRE 2410 Review of Interim Financial Information Performed by the Independent Auditor of the Entity

Scope: Auditor's professional responsibilities when the independent auditor of the entity is engaged to review interim financial information of that audit client, plus form & content of the review report. Key use-case in India: SEBI LODR quarterly review of listed companies.

Objective

"The objective of an engagement to review interim financial information is to enable the auditor to express a conclusion whether, on the basis of the review, anything has come to the auditor's attention that causes the auditor to believe that the interim financial information is not prepared, in all material respects, in accordance with an applicable financial reporting framework."

Key Definitions

  • Interim financial information — FI prepared & presented per applicable FRF; complete or condensed set of FS for a period shorter than the entity's financial year (e.g. a quarter)
  • Risk is reduced to a moderate level (not low as in audit) of expressing an inappropriate conclusion

Key Requirements — performance

  • Agree terms in engagement letter — scope, management & auditor responsibilities, assurance level, report form
  • Update understanding of entity & IC relating to both annual and interim FI — read prior audit & interim docs, consider significant risks (incl. management override), materiality, prior misstatements, internal audit results, management's fraud risk assessment, changes in business / IC
  • Perform inquiry + analytical + other review procedures — read minutes; inquire re changes in accounting policies, new transactions, uncorrected misstatements, unusual/complex situations, fair-value assumptions, related parties, commitments, contingencies, debt covenants, transactions near period-end, fraud/suspected fraud, NOCLAR; apply analytics to interim FI; read interim FI
  • Reconcile interim FI to accounting records (general ledger / consolidating schedule) and other supporting data
  • Inquire re subsequent events up to report date (no obligation post report-date)
  • Inquire re going concern assessment; if doubt arises, inquire about plans, their feasibility, and adequacy of disclosure
  • Obtain written representations — responsibility for IC to prevent/detect fraud-error; FI per FRF; uncorrected misstatements immaterial; disclosure of fraud, fraud-risk assessment, NOCLAR, subsequent events

Exceptions / Conditions

A review is not designed to obtain reasonable assurance. Ordinarily no tests of records by inspection, observation or confirmation. A lawyer inquiry letter is ordinarily not needed — but send one if something material surfaces and the lawyer would have pertinent info.
Do not accept the engagement if preliminary knowledge indicates management will impose a scope limitation preventing completion of the review.
If management does not respond appropriately to a required material adjustment → inform TCWG. If TCWG also do not respond → consider modifying the report, withdrawing from the engagement, and possibility of resigning from the annual audit appointment.

Evaluation of misstatements & accompanying information

  • Evaluate uncorrected misstatements — individually & in aggregate — against interim FI materiality
  • Read accompanying information for material inconsistency with interim FI; if inconsistency or misstatement of fact, discuss with management; if refused, consider additional paragraph, withholding report, or withdrawing

Conclusion / Reporting

  • Unmodified: "nothing has come to our attention that causes us to believe that the interim financial information does not give a true and fair view / is not prepared, in all material respects, in accordance with the applicable FRF"
  • Qualified / Adverse: when material adjustment needed and management does not correct — qualified (material); adverse (material & pervasive, qualified not adequate)
  • Disclaimer: limitation on scope preventing completion; if report required by law/regulation, disclaim and state reasons
  • EOM paragraph: material uncertainty on going concern where adequately disclosed; if not disclosed → qualified or adverse with specific reference to uncertainty
  • Report states: conducted under SRE 2410; inquiries + analytical + other review; "substantially less in scope than an audit conducted in accordance with SAs"; no audit opinion expressed
  • Include auditor's membership number, firm's registration number, and UDIN (mandatory for both SRE 2400 & 2410)

Other considerations

If management issues interim FI without the auditor's (especially modified) review report, consider legal advice and possibility of resigning from the annual audit. Condensed interim FI presumes users have access to latest audited FS — discuss with management the need to state that interim FI "to be read in conjunction with" the latest audited FS.

Documentation

Sufficient & appropriate to (i) provide basis for the conclusion and (ii) evidence that the review was performed per SRE 2410 and legal / regulatory requirements.

Procedure flow inside a review (common to 2400 & 2410)

Accept → determine materiality → understand entity & FRF → inquiry + analytical → specific-circumstance procedures (related parties, fraud/NOCLAR, going concern) → if something flags risk of misstatement → additional procedures → written reps → evaluate evidence → conclude (unmodified / qualified / adverse / disclaim) → report.

Why inquiry & analytical procedures are central

Inquiry is the principal source of evidence about management intent. Because corroborating evidence for intent is often limited, the practitioner leans on management's past history of carrying out stated intentions, the reasons for a chosen course of action, and the ability to pursue it. Professional skepticism in evaluating management's responses is essential to flag anything suggesting material misstatement.

Analytical procedures assist the practitioner in updating entity understanding, identifying inconsistencies / variances from expected trends or KPIs, providing corroborative evidence, and serving as additional procedures when misstatement is suspected. Methods range from simple comparisons to complex statistical techniques — e.g. comparative analysis of monthly revenue & cost across profit centres / branches / components.

Materiality in a review — the trap

Materiality for FS as a whole is determined and applied the same way as in an audit — the concept does not change with the assurance level. The practitioner uses it to design procedures and to evaluate results. If new information during the review would have led to a different initial amount, materiality is revised. Students often wrongly state that "review materiality is higher" — it is not; the procedures are fewer, not the yardstick.

Key takeaways (PDF §Key Takeaways)

  • Review = limited assurance = fewer procedures, lower level than audit, negatively worded conclusion
  • SQC 1 applies; engagement partner is responsible for quality of each review engagement
  • SRE 2400 — practitioner not the entity's auditor; SRE 2410 — entity's own auditor; procedures similar in spirit, but 2410 leans on the auditor's pre-existing understanding of the entity
  • Inquiry is principal source of evidence about management intent; analytical procedures identify inconsistencies & corroborate inquiries
  • Additional procedures kick in the moment the practitioner suspects material misstatement — can include substantive test of details / external confirmations
  • UDIN is mandatory for engagements under both SRE 2400 and SRE 2410
  • SEBI (LODR) Reg 33 mandates quarterly review of listed-company financial results by the statutory auditor — the canonical SRE 2410 use-case in India

5 Exam Triggers — spot these phrasings, invoke these SREs

  1. "Roma Ltd's contract with Dorma Ltd requires quarterly review of Roma's FS per the contract's financial reporting provisions; Roma is not audited by that practitioner" → SRE 2400 — review of special-purpose historical FS by a practitioner who is not the entity's auditor; issue review report with limited-assurance / negative-wording conclusion.
  2. "CA Aditya is statutory auditor of a listed company and is asked to perform the SEBI LODR quarterly review of its financial results" → SRE 2410 — update understanding using prior audit work; inquiries + analytical + other review procedures; report with "nothing has come to our attention" conclusion; include UDIN.
  3. "During review of Q2 results, analysis shows material past-due receivables with no allowance; management response unclear — practitioner cannot yet conclude whether FS are misstated or not" → SRE 2400 / 2410 additional procedures (deeper inquiry/analytics, substantive test of details, external confirmations); if still cannot conclude → scope limitation → qualified / disclaimer.
  4. "Quarterly review: major fire, machinery & inventory uninsured, massive disruption; matter adequately disclosed as a going-concern uncertainty in the interim FI" → SRE 2410 — add Emphasis of Matter paragraph; if not adequately disclosed → qualified or adverse conclusion with specific reference to the material uncertainty.
  5. "CA Seerat (auditor + reviewer) believes a material adjustment to quarterly FI is needed; communicated to CFO & Audit Committee; no response within reasonable time" → SRE 2410 options: (a) modify the review report, (b) withdraw from the review engagement, (c) consider resigning from the appointment to audit the annual FS.
