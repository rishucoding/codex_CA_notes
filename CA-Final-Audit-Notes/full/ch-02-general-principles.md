## Chapter map
This is the foundational chapter: it fixes the auditor's overall objectives (SA 200), the front-end preconditions and engagement terms (SA 210), what must be documented (SA 230), the auditor's fraud and law/regulation duties (SA 240 · SA 250), the governance-communication duties (SA 260 · SA 265), and the joint-audit mechanics (SA 299). SA 220 (firm-level QC on an engagement) lives in Ch 1 with SQC 1; SA 300/315/320/330 begin in Ch 3-4. In exam fact-patterns, these SAs are usually **the diagnosis** — the question tells a story and asks which of these duties the auditor breached.

## SA 200 — Overall Objectives of the Independent Auditor and the Conduct of an Audit in Accordance with Standards on Auditing

**Scope** (1 line): establishes overall objectives, nature & scope of an audit, and overarching conduct requirements · **Effective for:** audits of FS for periods beginning on or after **1 April 2010**.

**Objective** (ICAI verbatim, blockquote):
> "In conducting an audit of financial statements, the overall objectives of the auditor are: (a) to obtain reasonable assurance about whether the financial statements as a whole are free from material misstatement, whether due to fraud or error, thereby enabling the auditor to express an opinion on whether the financial statements are prepared, in all material respects, in accordance with an applicable financial reporting framework; and (b) to report on the financial statements, and communicate as required by the SAs, in accordance with the auditor's findings."

**Definitions** (only tested terms):
- **Reasonable assurance** — high, but not absolute, level of assurance.
- **Professional skepticism** — questioning mind, alert to possible misstatement.
- **Professional judgment** — training + knowledge + experience applied to decisions.
- **Applicable FRF** — FRF adopted by management, acceptable to the auditor.
- **Premise** — management's responsibilities for FS, internal control, access.

**Key Requirements** (keyword-form):
- **Ethical requirements** · independence · integrity · objectivity · confidentiality · professional behaviour · Code of Ethics.
- **Professional skepticism** · questioning mind · critical assessment · not assume honesty/dishonesty.
- **Professional judgment** · all phases · materiality, risk, sufficiency of evidence, opinion.
- **Sufficient appropriate audit evidence** · **audit risk to acceptably low** · IR × CR × DR.
- **Conduct per SAs** · comply with every relevant SA · read entirely including application material.
- **Objectives of each SA** · achieve; if not → **consider impact on opinion / modify**.

**⚠ Exceptions / Conditions:**
- ⚠ **Inherent limitations** — nature of FR (estimates), nature of audit procedures (sampling, mgmt override, collusion), timeliness/cost balance → **absolute assurance not attainable**.
- ⚠ Unable to achieve an objective of a relevant SA → **may require modification of opinion / withdrawal** (where withdrawal is possible under law).
- ⚠ Departure from a relevant requirement of an SA is permitted **only** where the requirement is conditional and the condition does not apply, **or** in exceptional circumstances with alternative procedures — document reasons.

**Auditor's Responsibilities:**
- Comply with ethical requirements including independence.
- Plan and perform with professional skepticism and judgment.
- Obtain sufficient appropriate evidence to reduce audit risk.
- Comply with SAs relevant to the audit.

**Management's Responsibilities** (the **premise**):
- Preparation of FS per applicable FRF.
- Internal control to enable FS free from material misstatement.
- Provide auditor access to information and unrestricted access to persons.

**Conclusion / Reporting:** Auditor forms an opinion on whether FS are prepared, in all material respects, in accordance with the applicable FRF.

## SA 210 — Agreeing the Terms of Audit Engagements

**Scope** (1 line): auditor's responsibilities in agreeing terms with management/TCWG · **Effective for:** audits of FS for periods beginning on or after **1 April 2010**.

**Objective** (ICAI verbatim):
> "The objective of the auditor is to accept or continue an audit engagement only when the basis upon which it is to be performed has been agreed, through: (a) establishing whether the preconditions for an audit are present; and (b) confirming that there is a common understanding between the auditor and management and, where appropriate, those charged with governance of the terms of the audit engagement."

**Definitions**:
- **Preconditions for an audit** — acceptable FRF + management's acknowledgment of the **premise**.
- **Audit engagement letter** — written record of agreed terms (see below).

**Key Requirements** (keyword-form):
- **Preconditions** · (i) acceptable FRF · (ii) management agreement to the premise (FS responsibility · IC · access).
- **Scope limitation imposed by management before acceptance** · ⚠ if so pervasive as to result in disclaimer → **do not accept** (unless law requires).
- **Agree terms in writing** · **engagement letter** · record agreement.
- **Contents of engagement letter** · objective & scope · auditor's responsibilities · management's responsibilities · applicable FRF · expected form/content of report · reference to SAs.
- **Recurring audits** · assess whether to revise terms · remind of existing terms.
- **Change in terms** · only with reasonable justification · ⚠ no reasonable justification → decline change · if unable to agree → **withdraw** where permitted and consider obligation to report.
- **Law/regulation prescribes terms** · still send engagement letter (confirming common understanding).
- **FRF prescribed by law + found unacceptable** · accept only if law requires AND management agrees to supplementary disclosures in FS AND auditor's report refers to the additional disclosures / uses EoM/OM or modifies.

**⚠ Exceptions / Conditions:**
- ⚠ Preconditions absent → **discuss with management**; if unresolved → **do not accept**.
- ⚠ Change from reasonable assurance audit to review/related service without justification → **do not agree**.
- ⚠ Initial audits: consider predecessor auditor communications (link to SA 510 opening balances).

**Auditor's Responsibilities:**
- Establish preconditions; agree terms in writing; re-evaluate on recurrence; resist unjustified change requests.

**Management's Responsibilities:**
- Use acceptable FRF; acknowledge the premise (FS · IC · access).

**Conclusion / Reporting:** Engagement proceeds only after terms — including management's premise and the auditor's scope — are documented and mutually accepted.

## SA 230 — Audit Documentation

**Scope** (1 line): responsibility to prepare audit documentation sufficient for understanding and evidence of basis for report · **Effective for:** audits of FS for periods beginning on or after **1 April 2009**.

**Objective** (ICAI verbatim):
> "The objective of the auditor is to prepare documentation that provides: (a) a sufficient and appropriate record of the basis for the auditor's report; and (b) evidence that the audit was planned and performed in accordance with SAs and applicable legal and regulatory requirements."

**Definitions**:
- **Audit documentation / working papers** — record of procedures performed, evidence obtained, conclusions reached.
- **Audit file** — physical/electronic folders containing the documentation for a specific engagement.
- **Experienced auditor** — individual (internal/external) with practical audit experience and reasonable understanding of audit processes, SAs, environment, FRF.

**Key Requirements** (keyword-form):
- **Timely preparation** · enhances quality and review.
- **Sufficient & appropriate record** · enable **experienced auditor with no prior connection** to understand nature/timing/extent (NTE) · results & evidence obtained · significant matters, conclusions, professional judgments.
- **Identification characteristics** · what was tested (sample items, dates, invoice numbers).
- **Discussions of significant matters** · nature · when · with whom.
- **Inconsistent information** · how auditor addressed it · document.
- **Departure from a relevant requirement of an SA** · reasons · alternative procedures performed.
- **Assembly of final audit file** · administrative process only · **within 60 days** from auditor's report date (per SQC 1).
- **Retention** · ⚠ **not shorter than 7 years** from date of auditor's report (per SQC 1).
- **Ownership** · working papers are **property of the auditor**; auditor may, at discretion, make portions available to clients.

**⚠ Exceptions / Conditions:**
- ⚠ After final assembly, **no deletion/discarding** before end of retention period.
- ⚠ Modifications after assembly permitted **only** if administrative; document who, when, why, and nature.
- ⚠ Exceptional post-report procedures (new facts / new audit evidence after the report date) → document circumstances, procedures, conclusions, effect on report.

**Auditor's Responsibilities:**
- Prepare documentation on a timely basis; maintain ownership and confidentiality; assemble and retain per SQC 1.

**Management's Responsibilities:** not applicable — documentation duty is the auditor's alone.

**Conclusion / Reporting:** Documentation is the auditor's evidence that SAs were complied with and the basis of the opinion; its absence is itself a qualification trigger.

## SA 240 — The Auditor's Responsibilities Relating to Fraud in an Audit of Financial Statements

**Scope** (1 line): expands SA 315 / SA 330 for fraud-related risks; deals with fraudulent financial reporting and misappropriation of assets · **Effective for:** audits of FS for periods beginning on or after **1 April 2009**.

**Objective** (ICAI verbatim):
> "The objectives of the auditor are: (a) to identify and assess the risks of material misstatement of the financial statements due to fraud; (b) to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence regarding the assessed risks of material misstatement due to fraud, through designing and implementing appropriate responses; and (c) to respond appropriately to fraud or suspected fraud identified during the audit."

**Definitions**:
- **Fraud** — intentional act by one or more among management, TCWG, employees or third parties, involving use of deception to obtain an unjust or illegal advantage.
- **Fraud risk factors** — events/conditions indicating incentive/pressure, opportunity, or attitude/rationalisation.
- **Two types ICAI tests:** **fraudulent financial reporting** (misstatements in FS) · **misappropriation of assets** (theft).

**Key Requirements** (keyword-form):
- **Professional skepticism** throughout · notwithstanding past honesty experience.
- **Engagement team discussion** · how/where FS susceptible · emphasis on skepticism · (SA 315 linkage).
- **Risk assessment procedures** · inquiry of management, internal audit, TCWG · analytical procedures · consider fraud risk factors.
- **Presumed risks** · ⚠ **revenue recognition = fraud risk** (rebut with documented reasons) · ⚠ **management override of controls = fraud risk in every audit**.
- **Responses to assessed risks** · overall (assignment, unpredictability, nature/timing/extent) · assertion-level · specific procedures for **management override**: test **JEs**, review **accounting estimates for bias**, scrutinise **significant unusual transactions**.
- **Written representations** · management acknowledges responsibility for prevention/detection; disclosures of known/suspected fraud & allegations.
- **Communicate** · to appropriate level of management (timely) · to TCWG (fraud involving mgmt or causing material misstatement) · to regulators where required by law.

**⚠ Exceptions / Conditions:**
- ⚠ **Reporting to Central Government under s.143(12) Companies Act** → ⚠ fraud ≥ ₹1 crore → **Form ADT-4** to CG within stipulated period after first reporting to Board/Audit Committee; < ₹1 crore → report to Board / Audit Committee; Board report (Directors' report) must disclose.
- ⚠ Fraud where auditor's ability to continue is in question → consider withdrawal (where legally permitted) and communicate to appointing authority.
- ⚠ Unable to determine whether suspected fraud / implications → seek legal advice; consider effect on opinion.

**Auditor's Responsibilities:**
- Plan with skepticism, evaluate fraud risks, respond, communicate, and report (including s.143(12)).

**Management's & TCWG's Responsibilities:**
- **Primary responsibility for prevention & detection of fraud** lies with TCWG and management · set appropriate tone · robust IC.

**Conclusion / Reporting:** Auditor's duty is to obtain reasonable (not absolute) assurance FS are free from material fraud-misstatement; where fraud is identified, pursue communication and, for companies, the s.143(12) reporting path.

## SA 250 — Consideration of Laws and Regulations in an Audit of Financial Statements

**Scope** (1 line): auditor's responsibility to consider L&R in an audit of FS · **Effective for:** audits of FS for periods beginning on or after **1 April 2009**.

**Objective** (ICAI verbatim):
> "The objectives of the auditor are: (a) to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence regarding compliance with the provisions of those laws and regulations generally recognised to have a direct effect on the determination of material amounts and disclosures in the financial statements; (b) to perform specified audit procedures to help identify instances of non-compliance with other laws and regulations that may have a material effect on the financial statements; and (c) to respond appropriately to non-compliance or suspected non-compliance with laws and regulations identified during the audit."

**Definitions**:
- **Non-compliance (NOCLAR)** — acts of omission or commission, intentional or unintentional, by the entity (or by TCWG, management, employees acting on its behalf) that are contrary to prevailing laws/regulations. Excludes personal misconduct unrelated to business.

**Key Requirements** (keyword-form):
- **Two categories** · **Direct effect L&R** (determination of material amounts/disclosures — e.g., tax, PF, CA provisions) → **obtain SAAE on compliance** · **Indirect / Other L&R** (operating licences, environmental) → **perform specified procedures**.
- **Specified procedures for indirect L&R** · inquiry of management/TCWG · inspect correspondence with regulators.
- **Obtain general understanding** of applicable legal & regulatory framework and how entity complies.
- **Remain alert** throughout for indications of NOCLAR.
- **Written representations** · all known/suspected NOCLAR disclosed to auditor.
- **Evaluate implications** of identified or suspected NOCLAR on risk and reliability of reps/evidence.
- **Communicate** · to TCWG (unless all on TCWG involved) · to management · report in auditor's report where material effect.
- **Report to authorities** · where required by law or if professional duty overrides confidentiality.

**⚠ Exceptions / Conditions:**
- ⚠ Suspected NOCLAR involving **TCWG** → communicate to **next higher authority** (e.g., audit committee, supervisory board); if no higher authority or auditor doubts action → **obtain legal advice**.
- ⚠ NOCLAR with material effect on FS, not adequately reflected → ⚠ **modify opinion** (qualified or adverse).
- ⚠ Scope limitation on determining whether NOCLAR has occurred → ⚠ **modify opinion** (qualified or disclaimer).
- ⚠ Auditor concludes NOCLAR intentional and material → ⚠ ability to rely on management representations is undermined; consider continuance.

**Auditor's Responsibilities:**
- Obtain SAAE on direct-effect L&R; perform specified procedures on other L&R; remain alert; communicate; modify report where needed.

**Management's & TCWG's Responsibilities:**
- **Responsibility for ensuring entity's compliance** with L&R rests with management, under oversight of TCWG (including prevention and detection of NOCLAR).

**Conclusion / Reporting:** Auditor reports material NOCLAR's effect on FS; where unresolved, modifies the opinion; where law requires, reports to regulator.

## SA 260 — Communication with Those Charged with Governance

**Scope** (1 line): two-way communication between auditor and TCWG in an audit of FS · **Effective for:** audits of FS for periods beginning on or after **1 April 2009**.

**Objective** (ICAI verbatim):
> "The objectives of the auditor are: (a) to communicate clearly with those charged with governance the responsibilities of the auditor in relation to the financial statement audit, and an overview of the planned scope and timing of the audit; (b) to obtain from those charged with governance information relevant to the audit; (c) to provide those charged with governance with timely observations arising from the audit that are significant and relevant to their responsibility to oversee the financial reporting process; and (d) to promote effective two-way communication between the auditor and those charged with governance."

**Definitions**:
- **Those charged with governance (TCWG)** — person(s)/organisation(s) with responsibility for overseeing strategic direction and obligations related to accountability (e.g., Board, Audit Committee).
- **Management** — person(s) with executive responsibility for conduct of the entity's operations.

**Key Requirements** (keyword-form):
- **Determine appropriate persons** within TCWG for communication (often audit committee).
- **Matters to communicate** · **auditor's responsibilities** (forming & expressing opinion — not relieving TCWG of theirs) · **planned scope & timing** · **significant findings** · **auditor independence** (for listed entities).
- **Significant findings** · auditor's views on significant qualitative aspects of accounting policies/estimates/disclosures · significant difficulties during audit · significant matters discussed with mgmt · written representations requested · circumstances affecting form/content of report · other significant matters relevant to oversight.
- **Independence communication (listed entities)** · statement of compliance · relationships/matters that may reasonably bear on independence · safeguards applied.
- **Form** · oral or written · significant findings **in writing** when oral communication inadequate · written communication retained in audit file.
- **Timing** · on a timely basis.
- **Two-way communication adequacy** · evaluate; if inadequate → consider effect on risk assessment, evidence, and possibly modify/withdraw.

**⚠ Exceptions / Conditions:**
- ⚠ Law restricts communication (e.g., legal privilege / ongoing investigation) → comply with law; document.
- ⚠ All TCWG involved in managing entity (e.g., owner-managed) → ensure matters communicated aren't lost; re-evaluate audience.
- ⚠ Communication delayed/ineffective → effect on audit and consider withdrawal (where permitted).

**Auditor's Responsibilities:**
- Determine audience, communicate required matters in writing/orally, document, evaluate two-way communication adequacy.

**Management's & TCWG's Responsibilities:** TCWG oversee financial reporting; this SA does not dilute their primary duty.

**Conclusion / Reporting:** Auditor's opinion is accompanied by timely, structured TCWG communication; persistent communication failure is itself an audit risk signal.

## SA 265 — Communicating Deficiencies in Internal Control to Those Charged with Governance and Management

**Scope** (1 line): auditor's responsibility to communicate deficiencies in IC identified during the audit · **Effective for:** audits of FS for periods beginning on or after **1 April 2010**.

**Objective** (ICAI verbatim):
> "The objective of the auditor is to communicate appropriately to those charged with governance and management deficiencies in internal control that the auditor has identified during the audit and that, in the auditor's professional judgment, are of sufficient importance to merit their respective attentions."

**Definitions**:
- **Deficiency in IC** — (i) a control is designed, implemented or operated in a way that it is unable to prevent, or detect and correct, misstatements on a timely basis; or (ii) a control necessary to prevent/detect/correct misstatements is missing.
- **Significant deficiency in IC** — a deficiency or combination of deficiencies that, in the auditor's professional judgment, is of sufficient importance to merit the attention of TCWG.

**Key Requirements** (keyword-form):
- **Determine deficiency** · evaluate individually and in combination.
- **Determine "significant"** — judgment factors: likelihood of material misstatement · susceptibility to loss/fraud · subjectivity/complexity · interaction with other deficiencies · volume of activity affected · importance to FR process.
- **Communicate significant deficiencies to TCWG** · **in writing** · on timely basis.
- **Communicate deficiencies to management** · at an appropriate level of responsibility · in writing (other than those already communicated to them by others) · on timely basis.
- **Written communication contents** · description of deficiency · potential effects · sufficient information to enable TCWG/mgmt to understand the context: (i) that the audit considers IC to design procedures, not to express an opinion on IC effectiveness; (ii) only those deficiencies of sufficient importance identified during this audit are communicated.

**⚠ Exceptions / Conditions:**
- ⚠ Auditor may communicate **orally** first if urgent; but significant deficiencies → **written** communication still required.
- ⚠ Clearly trivial matters → need not be communicated.
- ⚠ If deficiency already communicated in a prior period and not remediated → reiterate (do not omit because "already said").

**Auditor's Responsibilities:**
- Identify deficiencies, determine significance, and communicate in writing.

**Management's & TCWG's Responsibilities:** Management designs/implements IC; TCWG oversees; remediation is theirs.

**Conclusion / Reporting:** Communication to TCWG in writing of significant deficiencies is mandatory; this is distinct from, and not a substitute for, the opinion on FS.

## SA 299 (Revised) — Joint Audit of Financial Statements

**Scope** (1 line): principles governing responsibilities of auditors jointly engaged to conduct an audit · **Effective for:** audits of FS for periods beginning on or after **1 April 2018** (revised).

**Objective** (ICAI verbatim):
> "The objectives of the joint auditors are to: (a) lay down broad principles for the joint auditors in conducting the joint audit; (b) provide a uniform approach to the process of joint audit; (c) identify the distinct areas of work and coverage thereof by each joint auditor; and (d) identify individual responsibility and joint responsibility of the joint auditors in relation to the audit."

**Definitions**:
- **Joint audit** — an audit engagement where two or more auditors are appointed to jointly audit FS and issue a joint audit report.
- **Joint auditors** — the auditors so appointed.

**Key Requirements** (keyword-form):
- **Engagement acceptance** · communicate with retiring auditor (clause 8 First Sch. Part I) · agree on terms among joint auditors.
- **Division of work** · mutually by joint auditors · **before commencement** · documented · may be by items/areas of FS, units, periods, or any other basis.
- **Documentation of division** · written communication to entity · acknowledged by entity · retained in audit file.
- **Coordination** · bring to others' attention material observations in their areas that affect others' areas.
- **Audit planning, risk and materiality** · jointly establish overall audit strategy and materiality · jointly discuss RoMM at FS level.
- **Common work** (joint & several) · (i) audit work not divided · (ii) decisions taken jointly · (iii) matters brought to joint attention · (iv) examining that FS comply with statute and the applicable FRF · (v) ensuring the audit report complies with statute · (vi) presentation & disclosure of FS as required by FRF · (vii) determination and application of overall materiality.
- **Individual responsibility** · for work allocated to each joint auditor · each solely responsible for that portion.
- **Reliance on work of other joint auditors** · entitled to assume others have carried out their portion per applicable SAs; not required to review their work · but must exercise **due care**.
- **Reporting** · normally a **joint report signed by all**; ⚠ if any joint auditor disagrees → issue a **separate report**.

**⚠ Exceptions / Conditions:**
- ⚠ Disagreement on matter affecting opinion → separate audit report by the dissenting joint auditor.
- ⚠ Division of work not agreed in writing before commencement → SA 299 non-compliance; risk of entire audit becoming joint-and-several by default.
- ⚠ Joint auditor unable to perform own portion → replace or communicate impact to others; do not sign implying coverage.
- ⚠ Observations of fraud / NOCLAR in one joint auditor's area that affect others → **share promptly**; otherwise, those who did not receive the information are not liable for that work.

**Auditor's Responsibilities:**
- Agree and document division; do own portion diligently; share cross-cutting findings; jointly do overall strategy, materiality, compliance with FRF and statute, and FS presentation; sign joint report unless disagreeing.

**Management's Responsibilities:** Same as any audit — FS preparation, IC, access.

**Conclusion / Reporting:** Joint auditors are **jointly and severally** responsible for work done jointly and the overall report; **severally** responsible for divided portions. Dissent is expressed through a **separate report**, not silence.

## End-of-chapter: 5 Exam Triggers

Examiner fact-pattern phrasings that, when spotted, should immediately invoke the cited SA/clause. One line each.

1. **"Auditor accepted the engagement orally; management refused to acknowledge responsibility for internal control"** → invoke **SA 210** · focus on **preconditions absent** and duty to decline.
2. **"During inventory testing the auditor found unusual year-end journal entries moving revenue across periods"** → invoke **SA 240** · focus on **management override + revenue recognition presumed fraud risk** and JE testing; consider **s.143(12)** reporting if ≥ ₹1 crore.
3. **"Company's licence to operate was cancelled by the regulator mid-year and management did not disclose this"** → invoke **SA 250** · focus on NOCLAR with **material effect** → communicate TCWG and **modify opinion**.
4. **"After signing, auditor received information about a contingent liability and reperformed procedures but did not document the reasons"** → invoke **SA 230** · focus on **post-report modification** requirements and assembly-after-60-day rule.
5. **"Two joint auditors divided work orally and one discovered fraud but did not inform the other, who then signed a clean joint report"** → invoke **SA 299** · focus on **written division before commencement**, duty to share cross-cutting findings, and **separate report** where there is disagreement.
